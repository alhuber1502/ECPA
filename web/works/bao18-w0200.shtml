LINES

ON THE OPENING OF A SPRING CAMPAIGN.

1 SPRING! thy impatient bloom restrain!
2 Nor wake so soon thy genial power;
3 For deeds of death must hail thy reign,
4 And clouds of fate around thee lower: ....
5 In vain thy balmy breath to me
6 Scents with its sweets the evening gale;
7 In vain the violet's charms I see,
8 Or fondly mark thy primrose pale:
[Page 122]
9 To me thy softest zephyrs breathe
10 Of sorrow's soul-disparting tone;
11 To me thy most attractive wreath
12 Seems tinged with human blood alone.
13 Arrest thy steps, thou source of love,
14 Thou genial friend of joy and life!
15 Let not thy smile propitious prove
16 To works of carnage, scenes of strife:
17 Bid winter all his frowns recall,
18 And back his icy footsteps trace;
19 Again the soil in frost enthral,
20 And check the war-fiend's murderous chase.
[Page 123]
21 Fond, fruitless prayer! Thy hand divine
22 The smiling season on must lead;
23 And still at war's ensanguined shrine
24 Must bid unnumbered victims bleed.
