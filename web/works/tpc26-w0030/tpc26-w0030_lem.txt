address to the great

to the editor of the Lewes journal,

if i by pen, or counsel can
contribute ought unto
the nation’s good, i glad will
a work like that pursue:

but what, alas! will it avail,
what i can do or say,
my wish’d for end must sure fail,
while int’rest bear the sway.

while man of rank, and income great,
have int’rest so in view;
who have in yonder house a seat,
like slave, want wage too.

oh! cruelty, beyond compare!
that from that’ poor man’s labour
one half must go (though’ nought to spare)
to that’ rich ’squire his neighbour.

’Twas once a law the rich shall keep,
support, maintain the poor:
blush now you great! consider, weep!
and don’t increase your store

by aught forc’d from the low sort,
but vote some tax down,
which may bring credit to the court,
and safety to the crown.
