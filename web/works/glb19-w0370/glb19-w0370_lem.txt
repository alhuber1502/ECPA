on a beautiful landscape.

beautiful landscape! i can look on thou
for hour, unmindful of the storm and strife,
and mingle murmur of tumultuous life.
here, all be still as fair; the stream, the tree,
the wood, the sunshine on the bank: no tear,
no thought of time be swift wing, or close night,
that come to steal away the long sweet light —
no sigh of sad humanity be here.
here be no tint of mortal change; the day, —
beneath who light the dog and peasant-boy
gambol, with look, and almost bark, of joy, —
still seem, though century have pass, to stay.
then gaze again, that shadow scene may teach
lesson of peace and love, beyond all speech.
