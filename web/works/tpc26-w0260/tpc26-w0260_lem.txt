the british constitution

write by Thomas paine, on be ask by a Englishman for a few line on the subject by way of pastime.

of all the wonder man e’er see
that merit his applause,
since Adam delve in paradise,
and eve make bed of rose,
the great wonder of they all,
and free from all confusion,
it be — o lift your hand and eye —
the british constitution.
it be as stable as the hill,
that have endure forever;
it be as fleet as the wave
that glide in yonder river:
it be as change as a maid,
just when she be betrthe;
’tis this, ’tis that, ’tis either thing,
’tis every thing, ’tis nothing.
