shipwreck.

it be solemn midnight! on this lonely steep,
beneath this watch-tower's desolate wall,
where mystic shape the wanderer appal,
i rest; and view below the desert deep.
as through tempestuous cloud the moon cold light
gleam on the wave. viewless, the wind of night
with loud mysterious force the billow sweep,
and sullen roar the surge far below.
in the still pause of the gust i hear
the voice of spirit, rise sweet and slow,
and oft among the cloud their form appear.
but hark! what shriek of death come in the gale
and in the distant ray what glimmer sail
bend to the storm? — now sink the note of fear!
ah! wretched mariner! no more shall day
unclose his cheer eye to light you on your way!
