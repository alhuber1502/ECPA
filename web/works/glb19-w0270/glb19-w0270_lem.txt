association.

as over these hill i take my silent round,
still on that vision which be fly i dwell,
on image i love, alas, too well!
now past, and but remember like sweet sound
of yesterday! yet in my breast i keep
such recollection, painful though they seem,
and hour of joy retrace, till from my dream
i start, and find they not; then i can weep
to think how fortune blight the fair flower;
to think how soon life be first endearment fail,
and we be still mislead by hope smooth tale,
who, like a flatterer, when the happy hour
pass, and when most we call on she to stay,
will fly, as faithless and as fleet as they!
