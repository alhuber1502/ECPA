avenue in Savernake forest.

how soothe sound the gentle air that move
the innumerable leaf, high overhead,
when autumn first, from the long avenue,
that lift its arch height of ancient shade,
steal here and there a leaf!
within the gloom,
in partial sunshine white, some trunk appear,
stud the glen of fern; in solemn shade
some mingle their dark branch, but yet all,
all make a sad sweet music, as they move,
not undelightful to a stranger heart.
they seem to say, in accent audible,
farewell to summer, and farewell the strain
of many a lithe and feather chorister,
that through the depth of these incumbent wood
make the long summer gladsome.

to the deep-mingling sound of organ clear,
(when slow the choral anthem rise beneath),
the glimmer minster, through its pillar aisle,
echo; — but not more sweet the vault roof
ring to those link harmony, than here
the high wood answer to the light breath
of nature.
oh, may such sweet music steal,
soothe the care of venerable age,
from public toil retire: may it awake,
as, still and slow, the sun of life decline,
remembrance, not mournful, but most sweet;
may it, as oft beneath the sylvan shade
their honour owner stray, come like the sound
of distant seraph harp, yet speak clear!
how poor be every sound of earthly thing,
when heaven own music wait the just and pure!
