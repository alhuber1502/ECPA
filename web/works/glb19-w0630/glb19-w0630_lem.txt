come to these peaceful seat, and think no more
of cold, of midnight watch, or the roar
of ocean, toss on his restless bed!
come to these peaceful seat, you who have bleed
for honour, who have traverse the great flood,
or on the battle front with stern eye stand,
when roll its thunder, and the billow red
oft close, with sudden flash, over the dead!

oh, heavy be the sorrow that beset
old age! and hard it be — hard to forget
the sunshine of our youth, our manhood pride!
but here, o age man! you may abide
secure, and see the last light on the wave
of time, which waft you silent to your grave;
like the calm evening ray, that smile serene
upon the tranquil Thames, and cheer the sink scene.
