THE GENIUS OF THE MOUNTAINS OF BALAGATA,

IN THE EAST INDIES, BEWAILS THE MISERIES BOUGHT UPON HIS COUNTRY.

HIS SONG.

1 FROM Balagata's wavy brow
2 The Genius cast his eyes below,
3 Survey'd with grief the hostile plains,
4 And thus to Heaven address'd his strains.
5 Here first arose, in early time,
6 The beam of wisdom's light sublime;
7 From hence the stream of science flow'd,
8 Though now its source is lost in blood.
[Page 85]
9 O, Brama, where is now thy place?
10 And why on thy deserted race
11 Falls more than thy weak slaves can bear?
12 Behold them victims to despair!
13 Behold thy sons unpitied bleed,
14 While wealth and honours crown the deed:
15 See grasping avarice denies
16 A moment to their suppliant cries.
17 Dark deadly fraud, wild horrid strife,
18 The poison'd bowl, the murd'ring knife,
19 Combine thy people to annoy;
20 Rise and avenge them, or destroy!
21 With chains of fire the genii bind,
22 Who come the scourge of human kind;
23 Plunge them in seas of melting ore,
24 Crown them with poniards dip'd in gore.
25 O, Brama! may thy slave prevail,
26 Soon shall they spread their parting sail;
27 Command them distant far to fly,
28 Fell demons of calamity!
