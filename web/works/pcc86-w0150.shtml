Burntistand Castle, October 1764.

[IN shades! to pass the summer day]

1 IN shades! to pass the summer day
2 In Spring — to see the lambkins play,
3 In Autumn, western skies;
4 In each returning — season find
5 New beauties to amuse my mind,
6 Or gratitude to raise.
7 No morning ray! no setting fun,
8 No painted sky — shall be unsung,
9 With all its various clouds;
10 The gayest seat — adorn'd with art,
11 Or fields — or meads — their sweets impart,
12 With richest flocks and herds.
13 The peaceful cottage — in the glen,
14 With trees — around — and crystal well,
15 A chearful swain that sings.
16 All these — with equal mind I view.
17 From heaven descend the rains — and dew.
18 Thy wisdom all declare.
