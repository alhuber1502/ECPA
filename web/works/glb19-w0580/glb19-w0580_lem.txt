on leave Winchester school.

write in the year 1782. 

the spring shall visit thou again,
itchin! and yonder ancient fane,
that cast its shadow on thy breast,
as if, by many winter beat,
the bloom season it will greet,
with many a straggle wild-flower shall be dress.

but i, amid the youthful train
that stray at evening by thy side,
no long shall a guest remain,
to mark the spring revive pride.
i go not unrejoice; but who know,
when i have share, o world! thy common woe,
return i may drop some natural tear;
as these same field i look around,
and hear from yonder dome the slow bell sound,
and think upon the joy that crown my stripling year!
