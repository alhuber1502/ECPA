to governor Johnstone

when Satan first from heaven’s bright region fall,
and fix’d the gloomy monarchy of hell,
sin then be honest; pride lead on the tribe;
no devil receiv’d — no devil propos’d a bribe:
but each infernal, while he fight, abhorr’d
the mean mongrel art of sap and fraud;
brave in his guilt, he rais’d his dare arm,
and scorn’d the heaven, unless obtain’d by storm.
but Britain — oh! how painful ’tis to tell!
commit a sin that make a blush in hell;
low in the ruin of demolish’d pride
she bare skulk to conquer with a bribe,
and when detect in the rank offence,
throw out a threat — to turn King’s evidence.
yet while we scorn the lure, despite the plan,
we feel a angry sorrow at the man;
be there no wretch, who cold unkindl’d mind
ne’er know one gen’rous passion for mankind,
who hackney’d soul, the purchase of a pound,
no guilt can blacken and no shame confound?
no slave to act the dirty work — and spare,
from man of sentiment, the painful tear?
must Johnstone be the man? must he, who tongue
such able peal of locution ring,
who tow’ring genius seem’d at time to rise,
and mix a kindred fervor with the sky,
who point judgement, and connect sense,
give weight to wit, and worth to eloquence;
must he, oh shame to genius! by the first
to practise art himself so loud curse?
must he exhibit to a laugh mob,
a turn coat patriot conquer’d by a job;
and prove from under his adult’rous pen
how few be just of all the son of man?
when the sad echo of saint pulchre’s bell
toll to the cart wretch, a last farewell,
or when the tyrant see the lift steel,
they feel those pain which Johnstone ought to feel.
man may a while in infamy survive,
and by deception think himself alive,
but time will prove to his eternal shame
he die in earnest who outlive his fame.
of pit and you this contrast may be say,
the dead be live; and the live dead.
