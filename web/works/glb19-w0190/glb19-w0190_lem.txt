to a friend.

go, then, and join the murmur city throng!
i thou do leave to solitude and tear;
to busy fantasy, and bode fear,
lest ill betide thou; but it will not be long
ere the hard season shall be past; till then
live happy; sometime the forsake shade
remember, and these tree now leave to fade;
nor, 'amid the busy scene and hum of man,
will thou my care forget: in heaviness
to i the hour shall roll, weary and slow,
till mournful autumn past, and all the snow
of winter pale, the glad hour i shall bless
that shall restore thou from the crowd again,
to the green hamlet on the peaceful plain.
