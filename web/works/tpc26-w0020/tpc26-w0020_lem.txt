the monk and Jew.

a tale.

to make new convert true bless,
a recipe — probatum est.  —
stern winter, clad in frost and snow,
have now forbid the stream to flow;
and skait peasant swift glide,
like swallow, o’er the slippery tide:
when Mordecai (upon who face
the synagogue you plain may trace)
fortune with smile deceitful bear
to a curse hole, but late skinn’d o’er.
down plump the Jew, and sink find,
though’ deep the hole, the distant ground:
rise, the friendly ice he catch,
which keep he from the chill draught;
he gasp’d — he yell’d a hideous cry,
no friendly hand, alas, be nigh,
save a poor monk, who quick run
to snatch from death the drown man.
but when the holy father see
a limb of the mosaic law,
his hand outstretch’d he quick withdraw,
“for heav’n’s sake help!”  — exclaim the Jew.
“turn christian first” the father cry.
“I’m freeze to death”  — the Jew reply.
“freeze! quo the monk — too soon you’ll know,
“there’s fire enough for Jew below:
“renounce your unbelieving crew,
“and help be near”  — “i do — i do.”
“damn all your brother great and small”
“with all my heart,  — oh, damn ’em all:
“now help i out”  — “there’s something more,
“kiss this bless cross, and Christ adore;”
“there, there — i Christ adore,”  — “’tis well,
“thus arm, defiance bid to hell;
“and yet — another thing remain
“to guard against eternal pain;
“do you our papal father hold
“heav’n’s vicar? — and believe all tell
“by holy church?”  — “i do, by god,
“one moment more i’m food for Cod;
“drag, drag i out,  — i freeze,  — i die.”
“your peace my friend be make on high;
“full absolution here i give;
“saint Peter will your soul receive:  —
“wash’d clean from sin, and due shrive
“new convert always go to heaven;
“no hour for death so fit as this;
“thus — thus — i launch you into bliss.”
so say — the father in a trice
his convert launch’d beneath the ice.
