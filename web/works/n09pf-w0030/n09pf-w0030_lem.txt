art, science, and grammar rule;

enough to tire out Jew or Turk;
and make a christian bite his nail,
for do his best, he sure fail;
and spite of all that some may say,
his praise be trifle as his pay.

for my part i, though verse in book,
still save my carcase from such cook;
and always sly shun a trade,
too trifle as i think and say;
but at a certain crazy season,
when man have neither sense or reason:
by some confound misadventure,
i find myself just in it be centre.

and tense present, past and future;

where be my brain, or where be i?
the dull creature of the wood,
know how to shun the distant flood;
whale, dolphin, and a hundred more,
be not the fool to run ashore.

well, now content i must be,
force by the dame necessity,
who like the tribunal of Spain,
let we you speak once, but not again;
and swift to execute the blow,
never tell you why or whence it be so:

now i be ask a thousand question,
of Alexander and ephestion;
with fly design to know if i
be verse in grecian history;
and then again my time destroy,
with awkward grace to tell of Troy:
from that huge giant Polyphemus,
quite down to ROMULVS and Remus.
then i be oblige to give they lecture,
on quadrant, circle, square and sector;
or in my wretched memory bear,
what weigh a cubic inch of air.

"sir, here be my son, i beg you will mind,
the grace have be very kind,
and on he all their blessing shed,
[except a genius and a head]
teach he the doctrine of the sphere,
the slide circle and the square,
and starry world, i know not where:
and let he quick learn to say,
those learned word penna, pennae;
which late i hear our parson call,
as learning, knowledge all in all."

and there a city dame approach,
know by her horseman, chair and coach:
"sir, here be my son, teach he to speak
the hebrew, latin, and the greek:
and this i half forget, pray teach
my tender boy — the part of speech —
but never let this son of i,
learn that vile thing astronomy:
upon my word it be all a sham," —

and thus i be leave in street or road,
a laugh stock to half the crowd,

a plague i say on such employment,
where be neither pleasure nor enjoyment:
whoever to such a life be tie,
