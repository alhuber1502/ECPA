sonnet. [now the bat circle on the breeze of eve]

now the bat circle on the breeze of eve,
that creep, in shudder sit, along the wave,
and tremble 'amid the wood, and through the cave
who lonely sigh the wanderer deceive;
for oft, when melancholy charm his mind,
he think the spirit of the rock he hear,
nor listen, but with sweetly-thrilling fear,
to the low, mystic murmur of the wind!
now the bat circle, and the twilight dew
fall silent round, and, over the mountain-cliff,
the gleam wave and far-discovered skiff,
spread the grey veil of soft, harmonious hue.
so fall over grief the dew of pity tear
dim her lonely vision of despair.
