sonnet. [morn beam eye at length unclose]

morn beam eye at length unclose,
and wake the blush of the rose,
that all night long oppress with dew,
and veil in chilly shade its hue,
recline, forlorn, the languid head,
and sad seek its parent bed;
warmth from her ray the tremble flower derive,
and, sweet blush, through its tear revive.

"morn beam eye at length unclose,"
and melt the tear that bend the rose;
but can their charm suppress the sigh,
or chace the tear from sorrow eye?
can all their lustrous light impart
one ray of peace to sorrow heart?
ah! no; their fire her faint soul oppress —
eve pensive shade more soothe her meek distress!
