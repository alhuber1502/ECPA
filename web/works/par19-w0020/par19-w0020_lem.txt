sonnet, to the Lilly.

soft silken flower! that in the dewy vale
unfold thy modest beauty to the morn,
and breathe thy fragrance on her wander gale,
over earth green hill and shadowy valley bear;

when day have close his dazzle eye,
and die gale sink soft away;
when eve steal down the western sky,
and mountain, wood, and vale decay;

thy tender cup, that graceful swell,
droop sad beneath her chilly dew;
thy odour seek their silken cell,
and twilight veil thy languid hue.

but soon, fair flower! the morn shall rise,
and rear again thy pensive head;
again unveil thy snowy dye,
again thy velvet foliage spread.

sweet child of spring! like thou in sorrow shade,
full oft i mourn in tear, and droop forlorn:
and o! like thy, may light my gloom pervade,
and sorrow fly before joy live morn!
