Dover cliff.

on these white cliff, that calm above the flood
uprear their shadow head, and at their foot
hear not the surge that have for age beat,
how many a lonely wanderer have stand!
and, while the lift murmur meet his ear,
and over the distant billow the still eve
sail slow, have think of all his heart must leave
tomorrow; of the friend he love most dear;
of social scene, from which he weep to part!
oh! if, like i, he know how fruitless all
the thought that will full fain the past recall,
soon will he quell the rise of his heart,
and brave the wild wind and unhearing tide —
the world his country, and his god his guide.
