retaliation

i send you, sir, a tale about some fed’s
who, in their wisdom, go to loggerhead.
the case be this: they feel so flat and sink,
they take a glass together, and get drink.
such thing, you know, be neither new nor rare,
for some will hang themselves when in despair.
it be the natal day of Washington;
and, this they think a famous day for fun;
for, with the laugh world, it be agree,
the better day the better deed.
they tope away, and, as the glass go round,
they grow in point of wisdom more profound;
for at the bottom of the bottle lie
that kind of sense they overlook when wise.
come! here’s a toast, (cry one, with roar immense):
“may none know pleasure that love common sense.”
bravo! cry some; no, no, some other cry;
but leave it to the waiter to decide.
i think, say he, the toast will be more plain,
to leave out common sense, and put in paine.
on this a loud debate arise among
this job, sink, drunken throng.
some say, that common sense be all a curse,
that make people wise, make they worse;
it teach they to be careful of their purse,
and not be lead about like babe at nurse;
nor yet believe in story upon trust,
which all mankind, to be well govern, must;
and that the toast be better at first,
and he that didn’t think so, may be curse.
so, on they go, till such a fray arise,
as all that know what fed’s be, must suppose.
