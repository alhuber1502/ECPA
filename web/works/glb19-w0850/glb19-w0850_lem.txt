absence.

October 26, 1791. 

how shall i cheat the heavy hour, of thou
deprive, of thy kind look and converse sweet,
now that the wave grove the dark storm beat,
and wintery wind sad sound over the lea,
scatter the sallow leaf! i will believe,
thou, at this hour, with tearful tenderness
do muse on absent image, and press
in thought my hand, and say: oh do not grieve,
friend of my heart! at wayward fortune power;
one day we shall be happy, and each hour
of pain forget, cheer by the summer ray.
these thought beguile my sorrow for thy loss,
and, as the age pine their dark head toss,
oft steal the sense of solitude away.
so be i sad soothe, yet do i cast
a wishful glance upon the season past,
and think how different be the happy tide,
when thou, with look of love, be smile by my side.
