at Dover, 1786. 

thou, who stern spirit love the storm,
that, bear on terror desolate wing,
shake the high forest, or remorseless fling
the shiver surge; when rise grief deform
thy peaceful breast, hie to yond steep, and think, —
when thou do mark the melancholy tide
beneath thou, and the storm career wide, —
toss on the surge of life how many sink! 
and if thy cheek with one kind tear be wet,
and if thy heart be smite, when the cry
of danger and of death be hear more nigh,
oh, learn thy private sorrow to forget;
intent, when hard beat the storm, to save
one who, like thou, have suffer from the wave.
