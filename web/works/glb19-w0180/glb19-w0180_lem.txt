hope.

as one who, long by waste sickness wear,
weary have watch the linger night, and hear
unmoved the carol of the matin bird
salute his lonely porch; now first at morn
go forth, leave his melancholy bed;
he the green slope and level meadow view,
delightful bathe with slow-ascending dew;
or mark the cloud, that over the mountain head
in vary form fantastic wander white;
or turn his ear to every random song,
hear the green river wind marge along,
the while each sense be steep in still delight.
so over my breast young summer breath i feel,
sweet hope! thy fragrance pure and heal incense steal!
