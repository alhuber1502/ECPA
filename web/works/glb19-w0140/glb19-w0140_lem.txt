the convent.

if chance some pensive stranger, hither lead,
his bosom glow from majestic view,
temple and tower 'amid the bright landscape hue,
shall ask who sleep beneath this lowly bed? 
a maid of sorrow. to the cloister scene,
unknown and beautiful a mourner come,
seek with unseen tear to quench the flame
of hapless love: yet be her look serene
as the pale moonlight in the midnight aisle; —
her voice be gentle and a charm can lend,
like that which speak of a depart friend;
and a meek sadness sit upon her smile! —
now, far remove from every earthly ill,
her woe be bury, and her heart be still.
