on land at Ostend.

the orient beam illume the part oar; —
from yonder azure track, emerge white,
the early sail slow gain upon the sight,
and the blue wave come ripple to the shore.
meantime far off the rear of darkness fly:
yet 'amid the beauty of the morn, unmoved,
like one for ever tear from all he love,
back over the deep i turn my longing eye,
and chide the wayward passion that rebel:
yet boot it not to think, or to complain,
muse sad ditty to the reckless main.
to dream like these, adieu! the peal bell
speak of the hour that stay not — and the day
to life sad turmoil call my heart away.
