Poem Viewer

Analytical layer(s): Phonological
Visualization perspective(s): Analytical

Poem Viewer focuses on the specific but complex task of tracing the movement of sound through the defined space of almost any poem or poem fragment. This single poetic device involves twenty-six distinct variables, which have been mapped onto linguistic categories and visual channels. Readers can choose which linguistic variables to view — in isolation or relation — at any given time. Poem Viewer comprises two layouts: the default horizontal layout, which makes tracing relations easy (but requires horizontal scrolling), and a structured layout, which offers a number of animations tracking how sounds are formed in the mouth. Poem Viewer was a collaborative project between the University of Oxford and the University of Utah and was funded by the JISC/NEH's "Digging into Data Challenge" programme. [more]
