BARLEY BROTH.

Air — Crowdy.
1 IF tempers were put up to seale,
2 Our Jwohn's wad bear a duced preyce;
3 He vow'd 'twas barley i' the broth, —
4 Upon my word, says I, it's reyce.
5 "I mek nea faut," our Jwohnny says,
6 "The broth is guid and varra neyce;
7 I only say — it's barley broth."
8 Tou says what's wrang, says I, it's reyce.
9 Did ever mortal hear the leyke!
10 As if I hadn't sense to tell!
11 Tou may think reyce the better thing,
12 But barley broth dis just as well. "
[Page 214]
13 "And sae it mud, if it was there;
14 The deil a grain is i' the pot;
15 But tou mun ayways threep yen down, —
16 I've drawn the deevil of a lot!"
17 "And what's the lot that I have drawn?
18 Pervarsion is a woman's neame!
19 Sae fares-te-weel! I'll sarve my king,
20 And never, never mair come heame."
21 Now Jenny frets frae mworn to neet;
22 The Sunday cap's nae langer neyce;
23 She aye puts barley i' the broth,
24 And hates the varra neame o' reyce.
25 Thus treyfles vex, and treyfles please,
26 And treyfles mek the sum o' leyfe;
27 And treyfles mek a bonny lass
28 A wretched or a happy weyfe!
