commentary on the eastern wiseman travel to Bethlehem guide by a star to see the little Jesus in a manger

three pedlar trav’ling to a fair,
to see the fun of what be there,
and sell their merchandise —
they stop upon the road to chat,
refresh and ask of this or that,
that they may be more wise —

and pray the landlord say to they,
where go you sir? to Bethlehem,
the citizen reply
you be merchant sir to they say he,
we be reply the pedlar three:
and eastern man beside,

and pray what have you in your sack,
if worth the while, i will go snack?
to they quoth major domo,
we’ve button, buckle, spectacle,
and every thing a merchant sell
reply the travel trio.

these thing be very well say he,
for beaux, and those who can see,
much further than their knuckle,
but Bethlehem fair’ for boy and girl,
who never think of spectacle,
and can buy your buckle.

i have a pack of toy quoth he,
a travel merchant leave with i,
who can not pay his score.
and you shall have they on condition,
you sell they at a cheap commission,
and make the money sure.

There’s one of we will stay in pawn,
until the other two return,
if you suspect our faith say they,
the landlord thot this be a plan,
to leave upon his hand the man,
and therefore he say nay.

they truck’d however for the pack
which one of they take on his back;
and off the merchant travell’d
and here the tale, the apostle tell,
of wise man and, their gift of gold,
will full be unravell’d.

the star in the east, that shin’d so bright,
as may be see both day and night,
if you will credit they;
it be no other than a sign,
to a public house, where pedlar dine;
in east street Bethlehem.

the wise man be those pedlar three,
as you, and all the world may see,
by read to the end;
for commentator have mistake,
in paraphrase of a book,
they do not understand.

our traveller come to the house,
scarce fit to entertain a mouse,
inquir’d to have a room.
the landlord say he be not able,
to give they any but the stable,
so many folk be come.

and pray who have you there say they,
and how much money must we pay,
for we have none to spare.
why there one Joseph and a wench
who be to go before the bench,
about a love affair.

somehow or other in a manger,
a child expose to every danger,
be find as it be sleep.
the girl she swear that she’s a maid,
so swear the man and i’m afraid,
on i will fall the keep.

now if you’d set yourself about,
to find this knotty story out,
i’d pay whate’er it may be.
so in the travel pedlar go,
to pay their birth day compliment,
and talk about the baby.

they first unpack’d their pack of toy,
some for show and some for noise,
but most for the latter,
one give a rattle, one a whistle,
and one a trumpet make of gristle,
to introduce the matter.

one squak’d away, the other blow,
the third play’d on the rattle too.
to keep the bantling easy.
and thus the story come to we,
of which the people make such fuss,
about the eastern Magi.
