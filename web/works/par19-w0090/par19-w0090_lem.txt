Titania to her love.

o! fly with i through distant air
to isle that gem the western deep!
for laugh summer revel there,
and hang her wreath on every steep.

as through the green transparent sea
light float on the wave we go,
the nymph shall gay welcome i,
far in their coral cave below.

for oft upon their margin sand,
when twilight lead the freshen hour,
i come with all my jocund band
to charm they from their sea-green bower.

and well they love our sport to view,
and on the ocean breast to lave;
and oft as we the dance renew,
they call up music from the wave.

swift hie we to that splendid clime,
where gay Jamaica spread her scene,
lift the blue mountain — wild — sublime!
and smooth her vale of vivid green.

where throne high, in pomp of shade,
the power of vegetation reign,
expand wide, over hill and glade,
shrub of all growth — fruit of all stain:

she steal the sun-beam's fervid glow,
to paint her flower of mingle hue;
and over the grape the purple throw,
break from verdant leaf to view.

there myrtle bower, and citron grove,
over-canopy our airy dance;
and there the sea-breeze love to rove,
when tremble day depart glance.

and when the false moon steal away,
or over the chase morn do rise,
oft, fearless, we our gambol play
by the fire-worm radiant eye.

and suck the honeyed reed that swell
in tuft plume of silver white;
or pierce the cocoa's milky cell,
to sip the nectar of delight!

and when the shake thunder roll,
and lightning strike athwart the gloom,
we shelter in the cedar bole,
and revel 'amid the rich perfume!

but chief we love beneath the palm,
or verdant plantain spread leaf,
to hear, upon the midnight calm,
sweet Philomela pour her grief.

to mortal sprite such dulcet sound,
such blissful hour, be never know!
o fly with i my airy round,
and i will make they all thy own!
