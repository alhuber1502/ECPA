on the death of the reverend WILLIAM BENWELL, m.a.  

thou come with kind look, when on the brink
almost of death i strive, and with mild voice
do soothe i, bid my poor heart rejoice,
though smite sore: oh, i do little think
that thou, my friend, will the first victim fall
to the stern king of terror! thou do fly,
by pity prompt, at the poor man cry;
and soon thyself be stretch beneath the pall,
livid infection prey. the deep distress
of she, who best thy inmost bosom know,
to who thy faith be vow; thy soul be true,
what power of falter language shall express?
as friendship bid, i feeble breathe my own,
and sorrow say, pure spirit, thou be go!
