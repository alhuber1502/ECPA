to the vision of fancy.

dear, wild illusion of creative mind!
who vary hue arise to fancy art,
and by her magic force be swift combine
in form that please, and scene that touch the heart:
oh! whether at her voice you soft assume
the pensive grace of sorrow droop low;
or rise, sublime, on terror lofty plume,
and shake the soul with wild thrill woe;
or, sweet bright, your gay tint the spread,
bid scene of pleasure steal upon my view,
love wave his purple pinion over my head,
and wake the tender thought to passion true;
o! still — you shadowy form attend my lonely hour,
still chase my real care with your illusive power!
