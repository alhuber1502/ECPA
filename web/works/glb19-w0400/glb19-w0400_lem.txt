picture of a young lady.

when i be sit, sad, and all alone,
remember youth and love for ever flee,
and many friend now rest with the dead,
while the still summer light depart shine,
like many sweet and silent summer go;
thou come, as a vision, with a mien
and smile like those i once on earth have see,
and with a voice of that remember tone
which i in other day, long since, have hear:
like peace approach, when distemper fret
most the tire spirit, thy fair form appear;
and till i die, i never shall forget, —
for at thy footstep light, the gloom be cheer, —
thy look and voice, oh! gentle Margaret.
