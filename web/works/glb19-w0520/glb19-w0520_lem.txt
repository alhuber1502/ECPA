swift, brother, will thou run,

swift the delight to share,
the feast of joy that wait thou there.
swift, brother, will thou ride
over the long and stormy tide,
fleet than the hurricane,
till thou see those scene again,
where thy father hut be rear,
where thy mother voice be hear;
where thy infant brother play
beneath the fragrant citron shade;
where through green savannah wide
cool river silent glide,
or the shrill cicalas sing
ceaseless to their murmur;
where the dance, the festive song,
of many a friend divide long,
doom through stranger land to roam,
shall bid thy spirit welcome home!

fearless over the foam tide
again thy light canoe shall ride;
fearless on the embattle plain
thou shall lift thy lance again;
or, start at the call of morn,
wake the wild wood with thy horn;
or, rush down the mountain-slope,
overtake the nimble antelope;
or lead the dance, 'amid blissful band,
on cool andracte yellow sand;
or, in the embower orange-grove,
