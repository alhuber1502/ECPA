hymn to Woden.

god of the battle, hear our prayer!
by the lift falchion glare;
by the uncouth fane sublime,
mark with many a runic rhyme;
by the "weird sister" dread,
that, post through the battle red,
choose the slay, and with they go
to valhalla hall below,
where the phantom-chiefs prolong
their echo feast, a giant throng,
and their dreadful beverage drain
from the skull of warrior slay:
god of the battle, hear our prayer;
and may we thy banquet share!

save we, god, from slow disease;
from pain that the brave spirit freeze;
from the burn fever rage;
from wail of unhonoured age,
draw painful his last breath;
give we in the battle death!
let we lift our glitter shield,
and perish, perish in the field!

now over cumri hill of snow
to death, or victory, we go;
hark! the chief their car prepare;
see! they bind their yellow hair;
frenzy flash from their eye,
they fly — our foe before they fly!

Woden, in thy empire drear,
thou the groan of death do hear,
and welcome to thy dusky hall
those that for their country fall!

hail, all hail the godlike train,
that with thou the goblet drain;
or with many a huge compeer,
lift, as erst, the shadowy spear!
while hela inmost cavern dread
echo to their giant tread,
and ten thousand thousand shield
flash lightning over the glimmer field!

hark! the battle-shout begin —
loud sound the glorious din:
loud than the ice roar,
burst on the thaw shore;
or crash pine that strew the plain,
when the whirlwind hurl the main!
ride through the death-field red,
and single fast the destine dead,
see the fatal sister fly!
now my throb breast beat high —
now i urge my pant steed,
where the foeman thick bleed.
soon exult i shall go,
Woden, to thy hall below;
or over the victim, as they die,
chant the song of victory!
