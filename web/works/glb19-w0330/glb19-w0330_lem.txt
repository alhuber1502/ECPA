on accidental meeting a lady now no more.

write many year after the forgo sonnet.

when last we part, thou be young and fair —
how beautiful let fond remembrance say!
alas! since then old time have steal away
nigh forty year, leave my temple bare: —
so have it perish, like a thing of air,
that dream of love and youth: — we now be grey;
yet still remember youth enchant way,
though time have change my look, and blanch my hair,
though i remember one sad hour with pain,
and never think, long as i yet may live,
and part long, to hear that voice again; —
i can a sad, but cordial greeting, give,
and for thy welfare breathe as warm a prayer,
lady, as when i love thou young and fair!
