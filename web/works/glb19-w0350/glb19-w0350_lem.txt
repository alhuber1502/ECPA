woodspring abbey, 1836.  

these wall be build by man who do a deed
of blood: — terrific conscience, day by day,
follow, wherever their shadow seem to stay,
and still in thought they see their victim bleed,
before God altar shriek: pang succeed,
as dire upon their heart the deep sin lie,
no tear of agony can wash away:
hence! to the land remote limit, speed!
these wall be raise in vain, as vain flow
contrition tear: earth, hide they, and thou, sea,
which round the lone isle, where their bone repose,
do sound for ever, their sad requiem be,
in fancy ear, at pensive evening close,
still murmur miserere, domine.
