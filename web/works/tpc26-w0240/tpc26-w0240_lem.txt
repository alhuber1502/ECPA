line, extempore, by Thomas paine, July 1803

quick as the light’ning’s vivid flash,
the poet’s eye o’er Europe roll,
see battle rage — hear tempest crash,
and dim at horror’s threaten scowl.
mark ambition’s ruthless king,
with crimson’d banner scalp the globe,
while trail after conquest’s wing,
man’s fest’ring wound his demon’s probe.
Pall’d with the stream of reek gore,
that stain the proud imperial day,
he turn to view the western shore,
where freedom hold her bloodless sway.
’Tis here her rage triumphant sway,
a empire in the people’s love,
’Tis here the sovereign will obey,
no king but he who rule above.
