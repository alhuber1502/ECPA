Southampton water.

smooth go our boat upon the summer sea,
leave, for so it seem, the world behind,
its sound of mingle uproar: we, recline
upon the sunny deck, hear but the breeze
that over we whisper pass, or idle play
with the lithe flag aloft. a woodland scene
on either side draw its slope line of green,
and hang the water shine edge with shade.
above the wood, Netley! thy ruin pale
peer as we pass; and vecta azure hue
beyond the misty castle meet our view;
where in mid channel hang the scarce see sail. 
so all be calm and sunshine as we go
cheery over the briny element.
oh! be this little boat to we the world,
as thus we wander far from sound of care,
circle by friend and gentle maiden fair,
while morning air the wave pennant curl;
how sweet be life be long voyage, till in peace
we gain that haven still, where all thing cease!
