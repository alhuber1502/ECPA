evening.

evening! as slow thy placid shade descend,
veil with gentle hush the landscape still,
the lonely, battlement, the farther hill
and wood, i think of those who have no friend;
who now, perhaps, by melancholy lead,
from the broad blaze of day, where pleasure flaunt,
retire, wander to the ring-dove haunt
unseen; and watch the tint that over thy bed
hang lovely; oft to muse fancy eye
present fairy vale, where the tire mind
may rest beyond the murmur of mankind,
nor hear the hourly moan of misery!
alas for man! that hope be fair view the while
shall smile like you, and perish as they smile!
