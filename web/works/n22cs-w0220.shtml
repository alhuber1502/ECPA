SONNET [22] XXII. By the same. To Solitude.

1 OH, Solitude; to thy sequester'd vale[*]
[*]

LINE 1.

I climb steep rocks, I break my way through copses, among thorns and briars which tear me to pieces, and I feel a little relief.

Sorrows of Werter. Volume First.
2 I come to hide my sorrow and my tears,
3 And to thy echoes tell the mournful tale
4 Which scarce I trust to pitying Friendship's ears!
5 Amidst thy wild woods, and untrodden glades,
6 No sounds but those of melancholy move;
7 And the low winds that die among thy shades,
8 Seem like soft Pity's sighs, for hopeless love!
9 And sure some story of despair and pain,
10 In yon deep copse, thy murm'ring doves relate;
11 And hark! methinks in that long plaintive strain,
12 Thine own sweet songstress weeps my wayward fate!
13 Ah, Nymph! that fate assist me to endure,
14 And bear awhile — what Death alone can cure!
