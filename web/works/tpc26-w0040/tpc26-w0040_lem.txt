untitled

to the printer of the public advertiser.

behold! what martial band be those,
who look speak terror to their foe?
they mount the bark — they loose the sail,
and o’er the wide atlantic fly
with speed before the drive gale,
while hostile frown deform their eye.
what mean this more than frantic rage?
be headlong faction go so far?
can father with their son engage,
and Britain on her offspring war?
stop, stop, rash man, and timely learn to know
what dire effect from civil discord flow.
shall gallia’s son exult see
intestine broil our peace destroy?
or proud Spain, with envious glee,
by Faction’s aid indulge her joy?
forbid it, thou almighty lord!
that filial blood shall ever stain
Britannia’s conquer sword,
or blot with foul disgrace our reign.
no more, my son, with plaintive sound,
shall harsh strike my tortur’d ear;
hurt by oppression’s gall wound,
or drop in grief the silent tear:
henceforth we’ll try, with lenient hand,
gentle their fest’ring sore to heal;
if gentle sway and mild command
may yet restore the public weal.
be lawless rage restrain’d with chast’ning hand,
but not destruction deal on all the land.
speak thus, britannia’s gracious king,
and echo shout thy praise ring;
who loud acclaim shall rend the sky,
while rapid wind shall waft the sound
o’er the wide main, and spread the joy
thy discontent realm around:
then shall thy froward son forbear
their rash resolve — their bosom burn,
with honest shame they’ll drop the tear,
and soft affection soon return:
calm reason’s voice shall then be hear,
all harsh and jar discord cease,
misguided rage no more be fear’d,
but hush to harmony and peace:
with fervent zeal they then will raise
to gracious George affection’s voice;
with ardent love resound his praise,
and filial gratitude rejoice.
with rapt’rous heart they’ll join the festive day,
and joyful hail great george’s natal day.
persuasive be the muse, who honest aim
will humble point the path to genuine fame.
