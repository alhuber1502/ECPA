A Faithful Friend is the Medicine of Life. SON OF SIRACH.

1 IN the dreams of delight, which with ardour we seek,
2 Oft the phantom of sorrow appears;
3 And the roses of pleasure, which bloom in your cheek,
4 Must he steeped in the dew of your tears:
5 'Mid the fountain of bliss, when it sparkles most bright,
6 Salt mixtures embitter the spring,
7 Though its lustre may tremble through bowers of delight,
8 In the draught disappointment will sting.
9 But if Heaven hath one cup of enjoyment bestowed,
10 Unmingled and sweet as its own,
11 In the streams of affection its bounty hath flowed,
12 And there we may taste it alone.
[Page 249]
13 But the pure simple drops Love would seize as his prize
14 And defile them with passion's foul tide;
15 While the bowl he prepares as it dazzles our eyes
16 The poison of anguish can hide.
17 Let Friendship the stream, as it flows calm and clear,
18 Receive unpolluted for me;
19 Or if tenderness mingle a sigh or a tear,
20 The draught still the sweeter will be.
21 But let me reject the too-high flavoured bowl
22 Affectation or Flattery compose,
23 From Sincerity's urn thus transparent shall roll
24 The cordial of peace and repose.
25 Oh! give me the friend, from whose warm, faithful breast
26 The sigh breathes responsive to mine,
27 Where my cares may obtain the soft pillow of rest,
28 And my sorrows may love to recline.
[Page 250]
29 Not the friend who my hours of pleasure will share,
30 But abide not the season of grief;
31 Who flies from the brow that is darkened by care,
32 And the silence that looks for relief.
33 Not the friend who, suspicious of change or of guile,
34 Would shrink from a confidence free;
35 Nor him who with fondness complacent can smile
36 On the eye that looks coldly on me,
37 As the mirror that, just to each blemish or grace,
38 To myself will my image reflect,
39 But to none but myself will that image retrace,
40 Nor picture one absent defect.
41 To my soul let my friend be a mirror as true,
42 Thus my faults from all others conceal;
43 Nor, absent, those failings pr follies renew,
44 Which from Heaven and from man he should veil.
