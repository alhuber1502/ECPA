water-party on Beaulieu river, in the new forest.

i think it be a toy of the fancy, a dream
that lead with illusion the sense astray,
and i sigh with delight as we steal down the stream,
while the sun, as he smile on our sail, seem to say,
rejoice in my light, ere it fade fast away!

we leave the loud rock of ocean behind,
and steal along the clear current serene,
the Phædria spread her white sail to the wind,
and they who divide have many a day be,
gaze with add delight on the charm of the scene.

each bosom one spirit of peace seem to feel;
we hear not the toss, the stir, and the roar
of the ocean without; we hear only the keel,
the keel that go whisper along the green shore,
and the stroke, as it dip, of the feather oar.

beneath the dark wood now, as wind we go,
what sound of rich harmony burst on the ear!
hark, cheery the loud-swelling clarionet blow;
now the tone gentle die, now more mellow we hear
the horn through the high forest echo clear!

they cease; and no long the echo prolong
the swell of the concert; in silence we float —
in silence! oh, listen! it be woman sweet song —
the bend of the river reply to each note,
and the oar be hold drip and still from the boat. 

Mark the sun that descend over the curve of the flood!
seize, Wilmot, the pencil, and instant convey
to the tablet the water, the bank, and the wood,
that their colour may live without change or decay,
when these beautiful tint die in darkness away.

so when we be part, and toss on the deep,
and no long the light on our prospect shall gleam,
the semblance of one lovely scene we may keep,
and remember the day, and the hour, like a dream,
when we sigh with delight as we steal down the stream!
