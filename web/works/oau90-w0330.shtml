To CYNTHIA.

1 BY all the Sacred Powers I love ye so
2 There's nothing else so dear to me below;
3 And when your Cruel Scorn I would forsake,
4 Shunning the Rock that threatens me with wrack,
5 Some Angel stops my speed, and brings the Rover back.
6 Madam, my Heart no blemish yet has stain'd,
7 And never has deserv'd to be disdain'd,
8 Nor is it to be fool'd with ease,
9 But you may break it when you please,
10 Like melting Ore, your kindness makes it run.
11 But rigour turns it to a Stone,
12 And I had rather dye then see you frown:
13 So much your Influence you prove,
14 So much so tenderly I love,
15 And think not, dearest Saint, I can deceive,
16 But as you hope to be believ'd, believe;
[Page 130]
17 By Heaven and you my Life blooms or decays,
18 You point my wane or my encrease of days;
19 Fain, I confess, I would despair forget,
20 I would be bless'd if you thought fit,
21 Yet I too much your self-will'd Rigour fear,
22 For ah, what hopes is there of Love from her,
23 Whose very Soul is Love, and yet the word disdains to hear.
