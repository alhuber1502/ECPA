now evening fade! her pensive step retire,
and night lead on the dew, and shadowy hour:
her awful pomp of planetary fire,
and all her train of visionary power.

these paint with fleet shape the dream of sleep,
these swell the wake soul with please dread;
these through the gloom in form terrific sweep,
and rouse the thrill horror of the dead!

queen of the solemn thought — mysterious night!
who step be darkness, and who voice be fear!
thy shade i welcome with severe delight,
and hail thy hollow gale, that sigh so drear!

when, wrap in cloud, and ride in the blast,
thou roll the storm along the sound shore,
i love to watch the whelm billow, cast
on rock below, and listen to the roar.

thy mild terror, night, i frequent woo,
thy silent lightning, and thy meteor glare,
thy northern fire, bright with ensanguine hue,
that light in heaven high vault the fervid air.

but chief i love thou, when thy lucid car
shed through the fleecy cloud a tremble gleam,
and show the misty mountain from afar,
the near forest, and the valley stream:

and nameless object in the vale below,
that float dim to the muse eye,
assume, at fancy touch, fantastic show,
and raise her sweet romantic vision high.

then let i stand amid thy gloom profound
on some wild woody steep, and hear the breeze
that swell in mournful melody around,
and faint die upon the distant tree.

what melancholy charm steal over the mind!
what hallow tear the rise rapture greet!
while many a viewless spirit in the wind
sigh to the lonely hour in accent sweet!

ah! who the dear illusion please will yield,
which fancy wake from silence and from shade,
for all the sober form of truth reveal,
for all the scene that day bright eye pervade!
