the river Cherwell.

Cherwell! how please along thy willowed edge
erewhile i stray, or when the morn begin
to tinge the distant turret golden fan,
or evening glimmer over the sigh sedge!
and now repose on thy bank once more,
i bid the lute farewell, and that sad lie
who music on my melancholy way
i woo: beneath thy willow wave hoar,
seek a while to rest — till the bright sun
of joy return; as when heaven radiant bow
beam on the night-storm pass wing below:
whatever betide, yet something have i win
of solace, that may bear i on serene,
till eve last hush shall close the silent scene.
