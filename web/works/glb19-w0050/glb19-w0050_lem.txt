on leave a village in Scotland.

Clysdale! as thy romantic vale i leave,
and bid farewell to each retire hill,
where muse memory seem to linger still,
trace the broad bright landscape; much i grieve
that, mingle with the toil crowd, no more
i may return your vary view to mark,
of rock amid the sunshine tower dark,
of river wind wild, or mountain hoar,
or castle gleam on the distant steep! —
yet many a look back on thy hill i cast,
and many a soften image of the past
sad combine, and bid remembrance keep,
to soothe i with fair scene, and fancy rude,
when i pursue my path in solitude.
