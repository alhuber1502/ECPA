SONG.

I.
1 SLOW spreads the gloom my soul desires —
2 The sun from India's shore retires —
3 To EVAN'S banks with temp'rate ray,
4 Home of my youth! he leads the day.
5 O banks to me for ever dear!
6 O stream, whose murmurs still I hear!
7 All, all my hopes of bliss reside
8 Where EVAN mingles with the CLYDE.
[Page 160]
II.
9 And she in simple beauty drest,
10 Whose image lives within my breast,
11 Who trembling heard my parting sigh,
12 And long pursued me with her eye!
13 Does she, with heart unchang'd as mine,
14 Oft in the vocal bowers recline?
15 Or where yon grot o'erhangs the tide,
16 Muse, while the EVAN meets the CLYDE?
III.
17 Ye lofty banks that EVAN bound,
18 Ye lavish woods that wave around,
19 And o'er the stream your shadows throw,
20 Which softly winds so far below —
21 What secret charm to mem'ry brings
22 All that on EVAN'S border springs?
23 Sweet banks! — ye bloom by MARY'S side!
24 Blest stream! — she views thee haste to CLYDE!
[Page 161][IV.]
25 Can all the wealth of INDIA'S coast
26 Atone for years in absence lost?
27 Return, ye moments of delight,
28 With richer treasures bless my sight!
29 Swift from this desert let me part,
30 And fly to meet a kindred heart!
31 Nor more may aught my steps divide
32 From that dear stream which flows to CLYDE.
