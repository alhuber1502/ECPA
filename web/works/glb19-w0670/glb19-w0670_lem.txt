fountain, that sparkle through the shady place,
make a soft, sad murmur over the stone
that strew thy lucid way! oh, if some guest
shall haply wander near, with slow disease
smite, may thy cold spring the rose of health
bring back, and the quick lustre to his eye!
the ancient oak that on thy margin wave,
the song of bird, and through the rocky cave
the clear stream gush, their accord sound
shall mingle, and, like some strange music, steal
sad, yet soothe, over his ache breast.
and thou, pale exile from thy native shore,
here drink, — oh, can thou! — as of lethe stream!
nor friend, nor bleed country, nor the view
of hill or stream belove, nor vesper bell,
hear in the twilight vale, remember more!
