the river WAINSBECK. 

while slow wander thy sequester stream,
 Wainsbeck, the mossy-scattered rock among,
in fancy ear make a plaintive song
to the dark wood above, that wave seem
to bend over some enchant spot, remove
from life vain coil; i listen to the wind,
and think i hear meek sorrow plaint, recline
over the forsake tomb of he she love! —
fair scene, you lend a pleasure, long unknown,
to he who pass weary on his way; —
yet recreate here he may delay
a while to thank you; and when year have fly,
and haunt that charm his youth he will renew,
in the world crowd he will remember you.
