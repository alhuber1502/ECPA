song of the american indian.

stranger, stay, nor wish to climb
the height of yonder hill sublime;
for there strange shape and spirit dwell,
that oft the murmur thunder swell,
of power from the impend steep
to hurl thou headlong to the deep;
but secure with we abide,
by the wind river side;
our gladsome toil, our pleasure share,
and think not of a world of care.
the lonely cayman, where he feed
among the green high-bending reed,
shall yield thou pastime; thy keen dart
through his bright scale shall pierce his heart.

home return from our toil,
thou shall bear the tiger spoil;
and we will sing our loud strain
over the forest-tyrant slay!

sometime thou shall pause to hear
the beauteous cardinal sing clear;
where hoary oak, by time decay,
nod in the deep wood pathless glade;
and the sun, with burst ray,
quiver on the branch grey.

by the river craggy bank,
overhang with stately cypress-rank,
where the bush-bee hum his song,
thy trim canoe shall glance along.

tonight at least, in this retreat,
stranger! rest thy wander foot;
tomorrow, with unerring bow,
to the deep thicket fearless we will go.
