air. [now, at moonlight fairy hour]

now, at moonlight fairy hour,
when faint gleam each dewy steep,
and vale and mountain, lake and bower,
in solitary grandeur sleep;

when slow sink the evening breeze,
that lull the mind in pensive care,
and fancy lofty vision see,
bid music wake the silent air.

bid the merry, merry tabor sound,
and with the Fays of lawn or glade,
in trip circlet beat the ground,
under the high tree' tremble shade.

"now, at moonlight fairy hour,"
shall music breathe her dulcet voice,
and over the wave, with magic power,
call on echo to rejoice.
