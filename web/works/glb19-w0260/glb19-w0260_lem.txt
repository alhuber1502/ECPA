Netley abbey.

fall pile! i ask not what have be thy fate;
but when the wind, slow waft from the main,
through each rend arch, like spirit that complain,
come hollow to my ear, i meditate
on this world pass pageant, and the lot
of those who once majestic in their prime
stand smile at decay, till bow by time
or injury, their early boast forget,
they may have fall like thou! pale and forlorn,
their brow, besprent with thin hair, white as snow,
they lift, still unsubdued, as they will scorn
this short-lived scene of vanity and woe;
while on their sad look smile they bear
the trace of creep age, and the pale hue of care!
