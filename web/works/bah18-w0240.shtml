ELEGY TO THE MEMORY OF WILLIAM SEWARD, ESQ.

WHO DIED APRIL 24, 1799.

1 SAY, shall the muse, the muse to Seward dear,
2 Fail to the mournful rites her aid to lend?
3 Refuse to place a chaplet on his bier,
4 Or give a tear to her departed friend?
5 Ah no; she weeps! for in thy silent grave
6 The kindly mild affections wake no more;
7 Cold is that heart, where bounteous nature gave
8 Of warm benevolence her richest store.
9 Those powers by nature given, by time improv'd,
10 Still to some fair, some honest purpose led;
11 To cherish modest worth thy spirit lov'd,
12 To raise dejected merit's drooping head.
[Page 72]
13 The pride of learning, wit's resplendent ray,
14 The powers of genius, dazzling as they shine,
15 Before thy social virtues fade away,
16 Nor shall their loss be felt, or mourn'd like thine.
