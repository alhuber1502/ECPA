fairy sketch.

scene — Netley abbey.

there be a morrice on the moonlight plain,
and music echo in the woody glade,
for fay-like form, as of titania train,
upon a summer eve, beneath the shade
of netley ivy ruin, to the sound
of sprightly minstrelsy do beat the ground: —
come, take hand! and light move,
while our boat, in yonder cove,
rest upon the darken sea;
come, take hand, and follow i!

Netley! thy dim and desolate fane
have hear, perhaps, the spirit of the night
shriek, at time, amid the wind and rain;
or haply, when the full-orbed moon shine bright,
thy glimmer aisle have echo to the song
of fairy MAB, who lead her shadowy masque along.
now, as to the sprightly sound
of moonlight minstrelsy we beat the ground;
from the pale nook, in accent clear,
now, methinks, her voice i hear,
sound over the darksome sea;
come, take hand, and follow i!

here, beneath the solemn wood,
when faintly-blue be all the sky,
and the moon be still on high,
to the murmur of the flood,
to the glimpse of the night,
we perform our airy rite; —
care and pain to we unknown,
to the darken sea be fly.

hear no more life fretful noise,
heed not here pale envy sting,
far from life distemper joy;
to the water murmur,
to the shadow of the sky,
to the moon that ride on high,
to the glimpse of the night,
we perform our airy rite,
while care and pain, to we unknown,
to the darken sea be fly.
