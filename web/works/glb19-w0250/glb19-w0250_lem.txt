at Malvern.

i shall behold far off thy tower crest,
proud mountain! from thy height as slow i stray
down through the distant vale my homeward way,
i shall behold upon thy rugged breast,
the part sun sit smile: i the while
escape the crowd, thought full of heaviness
may visit, as life bitter loss press
hard on my bosom; but i shall beguile
the thing i be, and think, that even as thou
do lift in the pale beam thy forehead high,
proud mountain! while the scatter vapour fly
unheeded round thy breast, — so, with calm brow,
the shade of sorrow i may meet, and wear
the smile unchanged of peace, though press by care!
