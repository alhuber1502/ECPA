picture of a old man.

old man, i see thou in thy garden chair
sit in silence 'amid the shrub and tree
of thy small cottage-croft, while murmur bee
go by, and almost touch thy temple bare,
edge with a few flake of the white hair.
and, soothe by the faint hum of ebb sea,
and song of bird, and breath of the young breeze,
thus do thou sit, feel the summer air
blow gentle; — with a sad still decadence,
sink to earth in hope, but all alone.
oh! have thou weep to feel the lonely sense
of earthly loss, muse on voice go!
hush the vain murmur, that, without offence,
thy head may rest in peace beneath the churchyard stone.
