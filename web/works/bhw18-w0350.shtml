ELEGY ON A YOUNG THRUSH,

WHICH ESCAPED FROM THE WRITER'S HAND, AND FALLING DOWN THE AREA OF A HOUSE, COULD NOT BE FOUND.

1 MISTAKEN Bird, ah whither hast thou stray'd?
2 My friendly grasp why eager to elude?
3 This hand was on thy pinion lightly laid,
4 And fear'd to hurt thee by a touch too rude.
5 Is there no foresight in a Thrush's breast,
6 That thou down yonder gulph from me wouldst go?
7 That gloomy area lurking cats infest,
8 And there the dog may rove, alike thy foe.
[Page 223]
9 I would with lavish crumbs my bird have fed,
10 And brought a crystal cup to wet thy bill;
11 I would have made of down and moss thy bed,
12 Soft, though not fashion'd with a Thrush's skill.
13 Soon as thy strengthen'd wing could mount the sky,
14 My willing hand had set my captive free;
15 Ah, not for her who loves the Muse, to buy
16 A selfish pleasure, bought with pain to thee!
17 The vital air, and liberty, and light
18 Had all been thine; and love, and rapt'rous song,
19 And sweet parental joys, in rapid flight,
20 Had led the circle of thy life along.
21 Securely to my window hadst thou flown,
22 And ever thy accustom'd morsel found;
23 Nor should thy trusting breast the wants have known
24 Which other Thrushes knew when winter frown'd.
[Page 224]
25 Fram'd with the wisdom nature lent to thee,
26 Thy house of straw had brav'd the tempest's rage,
27 And thou through many a Spring hadst liv'd to see
28 The utmost limit of a Thrush's age.
29 Ill-fated bird! — and does the Thrush's race,
30 Like Man's, mistake the path that leads to bliss?
31 Or, when his eye that tranquil path can trace,
32 The good he well discerns through folly miss?
