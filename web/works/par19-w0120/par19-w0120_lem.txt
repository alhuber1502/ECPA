night.

over the dim breast of ocean wave
night spread afar her gloomy wing,
and pensive thought, and silence bring,
save when the distant water lave.
or when the mariner lone voice
swell faint in the pass gale,
or when the scream sea-gull poise
over the tall mast and swell sail,
bound the grey gleam of the deep,
where fancy form arouse the mind,
dark sweep the shore, on who rude steep
sigh the sad spirit of the wind.
sweet be its voice upon the air
at evening melancholy close,
when the smooth wave in silence flow!
sweet, sweet the peace its steal accent bear!
bless be thy shade, o night! and bless the song
thy low wind breathe the distant shore along!
