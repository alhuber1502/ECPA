story sonnet.

the weary traveller, who, all night long,
have climb among the Alps' tremendous steep,
skirt the pathless precipice, where throng
wild form of danger; as he onward creep
if, chance, his anxious eye at distance see
the mountain-shepherd solitary home,
peep from forth the moon-illumined tree,
what sudden transport to his bosom come!
but, if between some hideous chasm yawn,
where the cleave pine a doubtful bridge display,
in dreadful silence, on the brink, forlorn
he stand, and view in luna's dubious ray
far, far below, the torrent rise surge,
and listen to the wild impetuous roar;
still eye the depth, still shudder on the verge,
fear to return, nor dare to venture over.
desperate, at length the totter plank he try,
his weak step slide, he shriek, he sink — he die!
