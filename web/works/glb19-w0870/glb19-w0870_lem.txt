inscription.

come, and where these runnel fall,
listen to my madrigal!
far from all sound of all the strife,
that murmur through the walk of life;
from grief, inquietude, and fear,
from scene of riot, or of tear;
from passion, canker day by day,
that wear the inmost heart away;
from pale detraction envious spite,
that worry where it fear to bite;
from mad ambition worldly chase,
come, and in this shady place,
be thy contentment humble joy,
and a life that make no noise,
save when fancy, muse long,
turn to desultory song;
and wake some lonely melody,
like the water drip by.
come, and where these runnel fall,
listen to my madrigal!
