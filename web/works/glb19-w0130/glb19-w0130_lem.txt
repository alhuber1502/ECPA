influence of time on grief.

o time! who know a lenient hand to lay
soft on sorrow wound, and slow thence
(lull to sad repose the weary sense)
the faint pang steal unperceived away;
on thou i rest my only hope at last,
and think, when thou have dry the bitter tear
that flow in vain over all my soul hold dear,
i may look back on every sorrow past,
and meet life peaceful evening with a smile: —
as some lone bird, at day depart hour,
sing in the sunbeam, of the transient shower
forgetful, though its wing be wet the while: —
yet ah! how much must that poor heart endure,
which hope from thou, and thou alone, a cure!
