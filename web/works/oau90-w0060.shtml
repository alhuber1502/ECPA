A PARALLEL.

1 IN old Italian Prose, we read,
2 A youth by Riot and fond Love undone,
3 Had yet a Faulcon left of famous breed,
4 His chief diversion in his fatal Need,
5 And sole Companion when he left the Town.
6 The Saint that did his Soul possess,
7 Touch'd with a generous Sence of his distress
8 Made him a Visit at his poor retreat,
9 Whom his Heart nobly feasted, but, alas,
10 His empty Purse could get,
11 Nothing was good enough for her to eat:
12 Till rack'd with Shame, and a long fruitless search,
[Page 36]
13 He more to make his Love appear,
14 His darling Hawk snatch'd from the Pearch,
15 And dress'd it for his Dear,
16 Which generous Act did so intirely gain her,
17 She gave him all her Love and Wealth,
18 And nobly paid her Entertainer.
19 So when my Love with fate at strife
20 Had spent its whole Estate,
21 And Natures short-wing'd Hawk my Life
22 Was doom'd a Dish for Fate;
23 Divine Olympia chang'd the sad decree,
24 And with infallible Divinity,
25 Gave A new Being to my Soul and me.
