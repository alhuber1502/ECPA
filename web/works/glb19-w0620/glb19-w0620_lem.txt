in horto reverend J.  still,

apud KNOYLE, villam amœnissimam.

stranger! a while beneath this age tree
rest thou, the hill beyond, and flowery mead,
survey; and if nature charm may wake
a sweet and silent transport at thy heart,
in springtime, while the bee hum heedless nigh,
rejoice! for thou the verdant spot be dress,
circle with laurel green, and sprinkle over
with many a bud rose: the shrub all ring
to the bird' warble, and by fit the air
whisper amid the foliage over thy head!
rejoice, and oh! if life sweet spring be thy,
so gather its brief rose-bud, and deceive
the care and cross of humanity.
