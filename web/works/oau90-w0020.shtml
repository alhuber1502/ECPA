An ODE TO THE QUEEN.

I.
1 HIGH on a Throne of Glittering Ore
2 Exalted by Almighty Fate,
3 Out-shining the bright Jem she wore;
4 The gracious Gloriana sat.
II.
5 The dazling Beams of Majesty
6 Too fierce for mortal Eyes to see,
7 She veil'd, and with a smiling Brow,
8 Thus taught th' admiring World below.
[Page 20]
III.
9 Virtue is still the chiefest good,
10 And power, should only be her dress,
11 State, is a Fever to the Blood;
12 Free Conscience is the solid Bliss:
IV.
13 Glory is but a flattering Dream
14 Of Wealth, that is not, though it seem:
15 False vision, whose vain Joys do make
16 Poor Mortals poorer, when they wake.
V.
17 The fawning Crowd of Slaves, that bow
18 With Praise, could n'er my Sense controul;
19 Vast Pyramids of State seem low,
20 So much above it sits my Soul.
VI.
21 She spake, whilst Gods unseen that stood,
22 Admiring one so great so good,
[Page 21]
23 Flew straight to Heaven, and all along,
24 Bright Gloriana was their Song.
Returnel.
25 Bright Gloriana all along,
26 Bright Gloriana was their Song.
