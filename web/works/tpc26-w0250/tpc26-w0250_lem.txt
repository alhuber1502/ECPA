the strange story of Korah, dathan, and abiram, number, chapter xvi. account for.

old ballad sing of Chevy chase,
beneath who rueful shade,
full many a valiant man be slay,
and many a window make.
but i will tell of one much worse,
that happ’d in day of yore,
all in the barren wilderness,
beside the Jordan shore.
where Moses lead the people forth,
call’d choose tribe of god,
and feed they forty year with quail,
and rul’d they with a rod.
a dreadful fray once rise among
these self-named tribe of i be,
where Korah fall, and by his side,
fall dathan and abiram.
a earthquake swallow thousand up,
and fire come down like stone,
which slay their son and daughter all,
their wife and little one.
’Twas all about old aaron’s tithe,
this murderous quarrel rise,
for tithe be worldly thing of old,
that lead from world to blow.
a Jew of Venice have explain’d
in language of his nation,
the manner how this fray begin,
of which here be translation.
there be a widow, old and poor,
who scarce herself can keep,
her stock of goods be very small,
her flock one single sheep.
and when the time of shear come,
she count much her gain,
for now, say she, i shall be bless’d,
with plenty for my pain.
when Aaron hear the sheep be shear’d
and give a good increase,
he straightway send his tithe man,
and take away the fleece.
and this the weep widow go
to Korah, to complain,
and Korah he to Aaron go,
in order to explain.
but Aaron say, in such a case
there can be no forbear
the law ordain that thou shall give
the first fleece of thy shear.
when lamb time be come about,
this sheep become a dam,
and bless’d the widow’s mournful heart,
by bring forth a lamb.
when Aaron hear the sheep have young,
he stay till it be grow,
and then he send his tithe man,
and take it for his own.
again the weep widow go
to Korah, with her grief,
but Aaron say, in such a case,
there can be no relief.
for in the holy law ’tis write,
that while thou keep’st the stock,
thou shall present unto the lord,
the firstling of thy flock.
the widow then, in deep distress,
and have nought to eat,
against her will she kill’d the sheep,
to feed upon the meat.
when Aaron hear the sheep be kill’d,
he send and take a limb,
which, by the holy law, he say,
pertain unto he.
for in the holy law ’tis write,
that when thou kill a beast,
thou shall a shoulder and a breast,
present unto the priest.
the widow then, wear out with grief,
sit down to mourn and weep,
and in a fit of passion say,
the devil take the sheep.
then Aaron take the whole away,
and say the law record,
that all and each devote thing,
belong unto the lord.
the widow go among her kin:
the tribe of Israel rise,
and all the widow, old and young,
pull’d Aaron by the nose.
but Moses call’d a earthquake up,
and fire from out the sky,
and all the consolation be —
the bible tell a lie!
