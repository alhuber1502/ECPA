on the descent upon England, write at the desire of a favourite

1

blow soft you breeze o’er the wave,
soft as the thought that love inspire,
and safe bear, to angleterre,
the fleet that take my heart’s desire.
you gentle fate, if such there be,
who watch o’er human hope and fear;
o! land it safe and let i see
the friend that share my anxious care.

2

the world in tumult long have liv’d
a stranger to the voice of peace,
and long still will it be griev’d,
till britain’s crime be make to cease.
look round the globe from east to west,
or cast a glance from pole to pole
’Tis Britain bar the world from rest,
’Tis Britain harrow up the soul.

3

see afric’s wretched offspring tear
from all the human heart hold dear,
see million doom in chain to mourn,
unpitied even, by a tear.
see Asia and her fertile plain
where once the bramin dwell serene,
now, ravage by the thirst for gain,
till famine end the dismal scene.

4

it be to bind the tyrant hand,
to give the torture world repose,
that France send forth her choose band
to meet her friend, or seek her foe.
’Tis then, and not till then, that we,
a better world may hope to view,
’Tis then that man will happy be,
and nation love as i love you.
