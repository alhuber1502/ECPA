to melancholy.

spirit of love and sorrow — hail!
thy solemn voice from far i hear,
mingle with evening die gale:
hail, with this sadly-pleasing tear!

o! at this still, this lonely hour,
thy own sweet hour of close day,
awake thy lute, who charmful power
shall call up fancy to obey.

to paint the wild romantic dream,
that meet the poet muse eye,
as on the bank of shadowy stream,
he breathe to she the fervid sigh.

o lonely spirit! let thy song
lead i through all thy sacred haunt;
the minster moon-light aisle along,
where spectre raise the midnight chaunt.

i hear their dirge faint swell!
then, sink at once in silence drear;
while, from the pillared cloister cell,
dim their glide form appear!

lead where the pine-wood wave on high,
who pathless sod be dark see,
as the cold moon, with tremble eye,
dart her long beam the leaf between.

lead to the mountain dusky head,
where, far below, in shade profound,
wide forest, plain, and hamlet, spread,
and sad the chime of vesper sound.

or guide i where the dash oar
just break the stillness of the vale;
as slow it track the wind shore,
to meet the ocean distant sail:

to pebbly bank, that Neptune lave,
with measure surge, loud and deep;
where the dark cliff bend over the wave,
and wild the wind of autumn sweep:

there pause at midnight spectre hour,
and list the long-resounding gale:
and catch the fleet moonlight's power,
over foam sea and distant sail.
