on enter Switzerland.

languid, and sad, and slow, from day to day
i journey on, yet pensive turn to view,
where the rich landscape gleam with soft hue,
the stream, and vale, and hill, that steal away. 
so fare it with the child of the earth:
for when life goodly prospect open round,
their spirit burn to tread that fairy ground,
where every vale sound to the pipe of mirth.
but they, alas! the dream of youth beguile,
and soon a longing look, like i, they cast
back on the mountain of the morning past:
yet hope still beckon we, and beckon smile,
and to a bright world her view extend,
when earth long darkness on her path descend.
