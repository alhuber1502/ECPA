the pilgrim.

slow over the Apennine, with bleed foot,
a patient pilgrim wind his lonely way,
to deck the lady of loretto seat
with all the little wealth his zeal can pay.
from mountain-top cold die the evening ray,
and, stretch in twilight, sleep the vale below;
and now the last, last purple streak of day
along the melancholy west fade slow.
high over his head, the restless pine complain,
as on their summit roll the breeze of night;
beneath, the hoarse stream chide the rock in vain:
the pilgrim pause on the dizzy height.
then to the vale his cautious step he press,
for there a hermit cross be dim see,
crest the rock, and there his limb may rest,
cheere in the good man cave, by faggot sheen,
on leafy bed, nor guile his sleep molest.
unhappy Luke! he trust a treacherous clue!
behind the cliff the lurk robber stand;
no friendly moon his giant shadow throw
athwart the road, to save the pilgrim blood;
on as he go a vesper-hymn he sing,
the hymn, that nightly soothe he to repose.
fierce on his harmless prey the ruffian spring!
the pilgrim bleed to death, his eye-lid close,
yet his meek spirit know no vengeful care,
but, die, for his murderer breathe — a saint prayer!
