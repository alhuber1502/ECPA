song of a spirit.

in the sightless air i dwell,
on the slope sunbeam play;
delve the cavern inmost cell,
where never yet do day-light stray.

dive beneath the green-sea wave,
and gambol in the briny deep;
skim every shore that Neptune lave,
from lapland plain to india steep.

oft i mount with rapid force
above the wide earth shadowy zone;
follow the daystar flame course
through realm of space to thought unknown;

and listen to celestial sound,
that swell the air, unheard of man,
as i watch my nightly round
over woody steep, and silent glen.

under the shade of wave tree.
on the green bank of fountain clear,
at pensive eve i sit at ease,
while die music murmur near.

and oft, on point of airy clift,
that hang upon the western main,
i watch the gay tint pass swift,
and twilight veil the liquid plain.

then, when the breeze have sink away,
and ocean scarce be hear to lave,
for i the sea-nymph soft play
their dulcet shell beneath the wave.

their dulcet shell! i hear they now;
slow swell the strain upon my ear;
now faint fall — now warble low,
until rapture melt into a tear.

the ray that silver over the dew,
and tremble through the leafy shade,
and tint the scene with soft hue,
call i to rove the lonely glade;

or hie i to some ruin tower,
faint show by moon-light gleam,
where the lone wanderer own my power
in shadow dire that substance seem;

in thrill sound that murmur woe,
and pause silence make more dread;
in music breathe from below
sad, solemn strain, that wake the dead.

unseen i move — unknown be fear!
fancy wild dream i weave;
and oft by bard my voice be hear
to die along the gale of eve.
