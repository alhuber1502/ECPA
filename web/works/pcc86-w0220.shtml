Pitfirren, September —

EVENING.

Begs of a Painter to do justice to the following Groupe: — The beautiful Maria** Lady H — tt. with her two Infants, a Boy and a Girl. — One of them clinging round her Mother's neck at bed-time, near falls from her keeper's arms.

1 PRoud thus to wait, — each colour to prepare,
2 But wants the art — to paint the blooming fair.
3 Around her neck, in innocence she smiles,
4 And fondly — hides herself in infant wiles.
5 The maid obsequious — scarcely in her arms,
6 Restrains the babe — her slender hold alarms.
7 Choose then this groupe, dispos'd by softest shades
8 And playful win them to their evening beds.
[Page 53]
9 But how the mind — the mother to express?
10 Who fondly folds her infant to her breast.
11 A vain attempt — a figure far too fine —
12 A Raphael's hand could scarcely trace each line.
13 Steal fancy lightly — scarce the curtain by,
14 Nor breathe while sleep — the babes in slumber lie;
15 To the first cause let innocence my mind.
16 How moves the babe? — who forms the human kind.
17 They wake — the light — how joyfully he gues,
18 While fancy hovers as the two she views,
