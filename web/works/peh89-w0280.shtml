The WIDOWER's COURTSHIP.

1 Roger a doleful widower,
2 Full eighteen weeks had been,
3 When he, to meet the milk-maid Nell,
4 Came smiling o'er the green.
5 Blithe as a lad of seventeen,
6 He thus accosted Nell;
7 Give me your pail, I'll carry it
8 For you, if you think well.
9 Says Nell, indeed my milking-pail
10 You shall not touch, I vow;
11 I've carried it myself before,
12 And I can carry it now.
13 So side by side they walk'd a-while,
14 Then he at last did say;
15 My inclination is to come
16 And see you, if I may.
17 Nell understood his meaning well,
18 And briskly answer'd she;
19 You may see me at any time,
20 If you look where I be.
[Page 105]
21 Says he, but hear me yet a-while,
22 I've something more to tell;
23 I gladly wou'd a sweetheart be
24 Unto you, Mistress Nell.
25 A sweetheart I don't want, says Nell,
26 Kind Sir, and if you do,
27 Another you may seek, for I
28 Am not the lass for you.
29 When she had made him this reply,
30 He'd nothing more to say
31 But — Nelly, a good night to you,
32 And homeward went his way.
