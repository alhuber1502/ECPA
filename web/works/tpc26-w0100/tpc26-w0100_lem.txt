liberty tree

for the Pennsylvania magazine

in a chariot of light from the region of day,
the goddess of liberty come;
ten thousand celestial direct the way,
and hither conduct the dame.
a fair bud branch from the garden above,
where million with million agree,
she bring in her hand, as a pledge of her love,
and the plant she name, liberty tree.

ii.

the celestial exotic strike deep in the ground,
like a native it flourish’d and bear.
the fame of its fruit draw the nation around,
to seek out this peaceable shore.
unmindful of name or distinction they come,
for freeman like brother agree,
with one spirit endue, they one friendship pursue,
and their temple be liberty tree.

iii.

beneath this fair tree, like the patriarch of old,
their bread in contentment they eat,
unvex’d with the trouble of silver or gold,
the care of the grand and the great.
with timber and tar they old England supply’d,
and support her power on the sea;
her battle they fight, without get a groat,
for the honour of liberty tree.

iv.

but hear, o you swain, (’tis a tale most profane,)
how all the tyrannical power,
king, commons, and lord, be unite amain,
to cut down this guardian of we;
from the east to the west, blow the trumpet to arm,
through’ the land let the sound of it flee,
let the far and the near,  — all unite with a cheer,
in defence of our liberty tree.
