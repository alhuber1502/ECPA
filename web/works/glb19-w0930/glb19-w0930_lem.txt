on WILLIAM Sommers of Bremhill.

when will the grave shelter thy few grey hair,
o age man! thy sand be almost run,
and many a year, in vain, to meet the sun,
thy eye have roll in darkness; want and care
have be thy visitant from morn to morn.
while tremble on existence thou do live,
accept what human charity can give;
but stand thus, time-palsied, and forlorn,
like a scathe oak, of all its bough bereave,
god and the grave be thy best refuge leave.
when the bell ring, and summer smile ray
welcome again the merry Whitsuntide,
and all my humble villager be gay;
i see thou sit on the highway side,
to feel once more the warm sun bless beam:
do thou then think upon thy own gay prime,
on such a holiday, and the glad time
when thou be young and happy, like a dream
now perish! no; the murmur prayer alone
rise from the tremble lip towards the throne
of mercy; that ere spring return again,
and the long winter blow its dreary blast,
to sweep the verdure from the fade plain,
thy burden will be drop, thy sorrow past!
o blind and age man, bow down with care,
when will the grave shelter thy few grey hair!
