OXFORD revisit.

i never hear the sound of thy glad bell,
Oxford, and chime harmonious, but i say,
sigh to think how time have wear away,
some spirit speak in the sweet tone that swell,
hear after year of absence, from the vale
where Cherwell wind. most true it speak the tale
of day depart, and its voice recall
hour of delight and hope in the gay tide
of life, and many friend now scatter wide
by many fate. peace be within thy wall!
i have scarce heart to visit thou; but yet,
deny the joy seek in thy shade, — deny
each better hope, since my poor Harriet die,
what i have owe to thou, my heart can never forget!
