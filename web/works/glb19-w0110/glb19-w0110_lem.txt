the bell, Ostend. 

how sweet the tuneful bell' responsive peal!
as when, at open morn, the fragrant breeze
breathe on the tremble sense of pale disease,
so pierce to my heart their force i feel!
and hark! with lessen cadence now they fall!
and now, along the white and level tide,
they fling their melancholy music wide;
bid i many a tender thought recall
of summer-day, and those delightful year
when from a ancient tower, in life fair prime,
the mournful magic of their mingle chime
first wake my wonder childhood into tear!
but seem now, when all those day be over,
the sound of joy once hear, and hear no more.
