THE HAZARD OF Loving the Creatures.

I.
1 WHERE'ere my Flatt'ring Passions rove
2 I find a lurking Snare;
3 'Tis dangerous to let loose our Love
4 Beneath th' Eternal Fair.
II.
5 Souls whom the Tye of Friendship binds,
6 And Things that share our Blood
7 Seize a large Portion of our Minds,
8 And leave the less for God.
III.
9 Nature hath soft but powerful Bands,
10 And Reason She controuls;
[Page 79]
11 While Children with their little Hands
12 Hang closest to our Souls.
IV.
13 Thoughtless they act th' Old Serpent's Part;
14 What tempting things they be!
15 Lord, how they twine about our Heart,
16 And draw it off from thee!
V.
17 Our hasty Wills rush blindly on
18 Where rising Passion rolls,
19 And thus we make our Fetters strong
20 To bind our Slavish Souls.
VI.
21 Dear Sovereign, break these Fetters off,
22 And set our Spirits free;
23 God in himself is Bliss enough,
24 For we have all in thee.
