The FALL.

1 Ye Maids of Honour, mind your ways,
2 Nor wholesome counsel slight;
3 For oh! 'tis hard, in these our days,
4 To hold one's Dish upright.
5 By fate the strongest of us all,
6 And eke the steadiest too,
7 Are doom'd, or soon or late, to fall,
8 Nor are examples few.
9 The first of all the falling sex
10 Was Eve, our parent frail! —
11 Ah, Satan! Satan! thy sly tricks
12 Her daughters still bewail.
13 The next in fame, that made a trip
14 (O hear each Maid and Wife!)
15 Was Delia — and the only slip
16 She ever made in life.
[Page 119]
17 But fate foresaw the whole affair,
18 And plac'd before her eyes
19 A ball, three footmen, and a chair,
20 And eke a Beau likewife.
21 In such a case, what mortal Maid
22 In circumspection deals?
23 Or when a ball affects the head,
24 What nymph can mind her heels?
25 For, eager to be gone, 'tis said,
26 That morn she miss'd her pray'rs;
27 But vengeance swift o'ertook the Maid,
28 Alas! she fell down stairs.
29 Hence, ladies fair, with caution tread,
30 Be warn'd by Delia's slip;
31 And keep this maxim in your head —
32 To look, before you leap.
33 For she a Mourner is become,
34 Does penance for her sin;
35 And, 'stead of dancing, stays at home,
36 To weep a broken Shin.
