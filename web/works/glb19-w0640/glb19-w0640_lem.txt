to he, who, many a night upon the main,
at mid-watch, from the bound vessel side,
shiver, have listen to the rock tide,
oh, how delightful smile thy view again,
fair land! the shelter hut, and far-seen mill
that safe sail round and round; the trip rill
that over the grey sand glitter; the clear sky,
beneath who blue vault shine the village tower,
that high elm, sway in the wind, embower;
and hedge-row, where the small bird' melody
solace the lithe and loiter peasant lad!
o stranger! be thy pause fancy sad
at thought of many evil which do press
on wide humanity! — look up; address
the god who make the world; but let thy heart
be thankful, though some heavy thought have part,
that, shelter from the human storm' career,
thou meet innocence and quiet here.
