OXFORD REVISITED.

1 I never hear the sound of thy glad bells,
2 Oxford, and chime harmonious, but I say,
3 Sighing to think how time has worn away,
4 Some spirit speaks in the sweet tone that swells,
5 Heard after years of absence, from the vale
6 Where Cherwell winds. Most true it speaks the tale
7 Of days departed, and its voice recalls
8 Hours of delight and hope in the gay tide
9 Of life, and many friends now scattered wide
10 By many fates. Peace be within thy walls!
11 I have scarce heart to visit thee; but yet,
12 Denied the joys sought in thy shades, — denied
13 Each better hope, since my poor Harriet died,
14 What I have owed to thee, my heart can ne'er forget!
