ON A BEAUTIFUL SPRING,

FORMING A COLD BATH, AT COOMBE, NEAR DONHEAD, BELONGING TO MY BROTHER, CHAS. BOWLES, ESQ.

1 Fountain, that sparklest through the shady place,
2 Making a soft, sad murmur o'er the stones
3 That strew thy lucid way! Oh, if some guest
4 Should haply wander near, with slow disease
5 Smitten, may thy cold springs the rose of health
6 Bring back, and the quick lustre to his eye!
7 The ancient oaks that on thy margin wave,
8 The song of birds, and through the rocky cave
9 The clear stream gushing, their according sounds
10 Should mingle, and, like some strange music, steal[Page 99]
11 Sadly, yet soothing, o'er his aching breast.
12 And thou, pale exile from thy native shores,52
52 French priests, who have a residence near.
13 Here drink, — oh, couldst thou! — as of Lethe's stream!
14 Nor friends, nor bleeding country, nor the views
15 Of hills or streams beloved, nor vesper bell,
16 Heard in the twilight vale, remember more!
