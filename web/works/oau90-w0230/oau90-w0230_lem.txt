epsom-well: a satyr by way of dialogue, between critic and fame.

i 

Crit. 

fame, that do over the universe scatter
satyr and libel, and politic tell
say who be in the country drink the water;
and first begin with Epsom well. 

ii.

Crit. 

who be that lad there puff and sweat?
and who those rake hell that buz in his ear?

fame.

it be the mad lord that love the bul-baiting,
with all his brother dog and bear.

iii.

Crit. 

who be those two lank tallow face doxy,
that look as just they from sweat do crawl?

fame.

two London whore will wash off their pox,
dread their doom when leaf do fall.

iv.

cr. 

what city wife there on the down roll,
who with young bully to Box-Hill repair,

Fa. 

one, who while husband lose at Bowling,
take the right way to get he heir.

v. 

cr. 

but among all these, prithee dear rumour
what Iack in the box be that with coach and four?

Fa. 

a pox upon he, it be a perfumer,
that make a stink all Fleet-street over.

vi.

cr. 

what lady bright come yonder a tune,
to who the wit and the wittal so throng?

Fa. 

one that for all the rook be too cunning,
and play and sing all summer long.

vii.

cr. 

what bonny blade sit there above fifty,
chew the cud among elmor calf?

Fa. 

it be a old bachelor, that to be thrifty,
purchase land by full and half.

viii.

cr. 

the vicar here love wine above water,
cheere his heart against wofull time;

Fa. 

then coak the justice, and kiss his daughter,
there no more subject leave for rhime.
