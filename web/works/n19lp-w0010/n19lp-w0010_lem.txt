[bar fight, august 25, 1746, in] negro slavery in old Deerfield

“august it be the twenty-fifth
seventeen hundred forty-six
the indian do in ambush lie
some very valiant man to slay
the name of who i will not leave out
SAMUEL Allen like a hero fout
and though he be so brave and bold
his face no more shall we behold
Eleazer hawk be kill outright
before he have time to fight
before he do the indian see
be shoot and kill immediate
Oliver Amsden he be slay
which cause his friend much grief and pain
Simeon Amsden they find dead
not many rod off from his head.
Adonijah Gillet, we do hear
do lose his life which be so dear
John saddler flee across the water
and so escape the dreadful slaughter
Eunice Allen see the indian come
and hope to save herself by run
and have not her petticoat stop she
the awful creature have not catch she
and tommyhawk she on the head
and leave she on the ground for dead.
young SAMUEL Allen, oh! lack-a-day
be take and carry to Canada.”
