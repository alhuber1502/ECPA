* This little poem was, among several others on the same occasion, printed by Dr. Rogers, with his inaugural exercise at Padua; and afterwards in the same manner re-published by him at London, together with his Harveian oration before the college of physicians, in the year 1682; while Mr. Waller was yet living.
VERSES to Dr. GEORGE ROGERS,

on his taking the Degree of Doctor in Physic at Padua, in the Year 1664.

By Mr. WALLER.
1 WHEN as of old the earth's bold children strove,
2 With hills on hills, to scale the throne of Jove;
3 Pallas and Mars stood by their sovereign's side,
4 And their bright arms in his defence employ'd:
5 While the wife Phoebus, Hermes, and the rest,
6 Who joy in peace and love the Muses best,
7 Descending from their so distemper'd seat,
8 Our groves and meadows chose for their retreat.
9 There first Apollo tried the various use
10 Of herbs, and learn'd the virtue of their juice,
11 And fram'd that art, to which who can pretend
12 A juster title than our noble friend,
13 Whom the like tempest drives from his abode,
14 And like employment entertains abroad?
15 This crowns him here; and, in the bays so earn'd,
16 His country's honour is no less concern'd;
17 Since it appears, not all the English rave,
18 To ruin bent: some study how to save.
19 And as Hippocrates did once extend
20 His sacred art, whole cities to amend;
[Page 110]
21 So we, brave friend, suppose that thy great skill,
22 Thy gentle mind, and fair example will,
23 At thy return, reclaim our frantic isle,
24 Their spirits calm; and peace again shall smile.
EDM. WALLER, Anglus. Patavii, typis Pauli Frambotti.
