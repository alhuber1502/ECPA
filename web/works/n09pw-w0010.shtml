An ELEGIAC POEM, On the DEATH of that celebrated Divine, and eminent Servant of JESUS CHRIST, the late Reverend, and pious GEORGE WHITEFIELD, Chaplain to the Right Honourable the Countess of HUNTINGDON, &c &c. Who made his Exit from this transitory State, to dwell in the celestial Realms of Bliss, on LORD's-Day, 30th of September, 1770, when he was seiz'd with a Fit of the Asthma, at NEWBURY-PORT, near BOSTON, in NEW-ENGLAND. In which is a Condolatory Address to His truly noble Benefactress the worthy and pious Lady HUNTINGDON, — and the Orphan-Children in GEORGIA; who, with many Thousands, are left, by the Death of this great Man, to lament the Loss of a Father, Friend, and Benefactor.

By PHILLIS, a Servant Girl of 17 Years of Age, belonging to Mr. J. WHEATLEY, of BOSTON: — And has been but 9 Years in this Country from Africa.
1 HAIL happy Saint on thy immortal throne!
2 To thee complaints of grievance are unknown;
3 We hear no more the music of thy tongue,
4 Thy wonted auditories cease to throng.
5 Thy lessons in unequal'd accents flow'd!
6 While emulation in each bosom glow'd;
7 Thou didst, in strains of eloquence refin'd,
8 Inflame the soul, and captivate the mind.
9 Unhappy we, the setting Sun deplore!
10 Which once was splendid, but it shines no more.
11 He leaves this earth for Heaven's unmeasur'd height:
12 And worlds unknown, receive him from our sight;
13 There WHITEFIELD wings, with rapid course his way,
14 And sails to Zion, through vast seas of day.
15 When his AMERICANS were burden'd sore,
16 When streets were crimson'd with their guiltless gore!
17 Unrival'd friendship in his breast now strove:
18 The fruit thereof was charity and love
19 Towards America — couldst thou do more
20 Than leave thy native home, the British shore,
21 To cross the great Atlantic's wat'ry road,
22 To see America's distress'd abode?
23 Thy prayers, great Saint, and thy incessant cries,
24 Have pierc'd the bosom of thy native skies!
25 Thou moon hast seen, and ye bright stars of light
26 Have witness been of his requests by night!
27 He pray'd that grace in every heart might dwell:
28 He long'd to see America excell;
29 He charg'd its youth to let the grace divine
30 Arise, and in their future actions shine;
31 He offer'd THAT he did himself receive,
32 A greater gift not GOD himself can give:
33 He urg'd the need of HIM to every one;
34 It was no less than GOD's co-equal SON!
35 Take HIM ye wretched for your only good;
36 Take HIM ye starving souls to be your food.
37 Ye thirsty, come to this life giving stream:
38 Ye Preachers, take him for your joyful theme:
39 Take HIM, "my dear AMERICANS," he said,
40 Be your complaints in his kind bosom laid:
41 Take HIM ye Africans, he longs for you;
42 Impartial SAVIOUR, is his title due;
43 If you will chuse to walk in grace's road,
44 You shall be sons, and kings, and priests to GOD.
45 Great COUNTESS! we Americans revere
46 Thy name, and thus condole thy grief sincere:
47 We mourn with thee, that TOMB obscurely plac'd,
48 In which thy Chaplain undisturb'd doth rest.
49 New-England sure, doth feel the ORPHAN's smart;
50 Reveals the true sensations of his heart:
51 Since this fair Sun, withdraws his golden rays,
52 No more to brighten these distressful days!
53 His lonely Tabernacle, sees no more
54 A WHITEFIELD landing on the British shore:
55 Then let us view him in yon azure skies:
56 Let every mind with this lov'd object rise.
57 No more can he exert his lab'ring breath,
58 Seiz'd by the cruel messenger of death.
59 What can his dear AMERICA return?
60 But drop a tear upon his happy urn,
61 Thou tomb, shalt safe retain thy sacred trust,
62 Till life divine re-animate his dust.
