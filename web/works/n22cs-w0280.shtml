SONNET [28] XXVIII. To Friendship.

1 OH thou! whose name too often is profan'd!
2 Whose charms, celestial! few have hearts to feel!
3 Unknown to Folly — and by Pride disdain'd!
4 — To thy soft solace may my sorrows steal!
5 Like the fair Moon, thy mild and genuine ray,
6 Thro' life's long evening shall unclouded last;
7 While Pleasure's frail attachments fleet away,
8 As fades the rainbow from the northern blast!
9 Tis thine, oh Nymph! with 'balmy hands to bind'[*]
[*]

LINE 9. Balmy hand to bind. Collins.

10 The wounds inflicted in Misfortune's storm,
11 And blunt severe Affliction's sharpest dart!
12 — 'Tis thy pure spirit warms my Anna's mind,
13 Beams thro' the pensive softness of her form,
14 And holds its altar — on her spotless heart!
