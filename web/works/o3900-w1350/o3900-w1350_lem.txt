at meal.

i 

father, our eye we lift to thou,
 and taste our daily bread:
 it be now thy open hand we see,
 and on thy bounty feed. 

ii.

it be now the mean creature join
 rich thy grace to prove;
 fulfil thy primitive design,
 enjoy by thankful love. 

iii.

still, while our mouth be fill with good,
 our soul to thou we raise;
 our soul partake of noble food,
 and banquet on thy praise. 

iv.

yet high still our farther aim;
 to mingle with the bless,
 the attend the marriage of the lamb,
 and heaven eternal feast.
