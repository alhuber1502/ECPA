TO THE MEMORY OF THOMAS CHATTERTON.

1 ILL-fated youth! thy ardent soul
2 Aim'd at the heights of deathless fame,
3 Sprang from beneath the world's controul,
4 And seiz'd unknown a poet's name.
5 O that some friendly hand had deign'd to guide
6 Thy genius in its course! and sooth'd thy erring pride.
7 I mark thy muse; her gothic lyre
8 Well suits the legendary lay;
9 While darting from her eyes of fire
10 She beams a visionary day:
11 Bright as the magic torch she early gave
12 To light thy vent'rous way, through fancy's secret cave.
[Page 22]
13 There, as she taught thee to behold
14 Imagin'd deeds of distant years,
15 Embattled knights and barons bold,
16 Great Ella's griefs, or Juga's tears;
17 Rapid as thought arose the glowing scene,
18 Till poverty, despair, and death, rush'd in between.
19 Poet sublime! although no sculptur'd urn,
20 No monumental bust thy ashes grace;
21 No fair inscription teaches whom to mourn,
22 No cypress shades the consecrated place,
23 Thy name shall live on time's recording page,
24 The wonder and reproach of an enlighten'd age.
