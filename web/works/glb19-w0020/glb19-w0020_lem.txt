Bamborough castle. 

you holy tower that shade the wave-worn steep,
long may you rear your age brow sublime,
though, hurry silent by, relentless time
assail you, and the wind of winter sweep
round your dark battlement; for far from hall
of pride, here charity have fix her seat,
oft listen, tearful, when the tempest beat
with hollow bode round your ancient wall;
and pity, at the dark and stormy hour
of midnight, when the moon be hide on high,
keep her lone watch upon the topmost tower,
and turn her ear to each expire cry;
bless if her aid some faint wretch may save,
and snatch he cold and speechless from the wave.
