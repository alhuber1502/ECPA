sun-dial, in the churchyard of Bremhill.

so pass silent over the dead thy shade,
brief time; and hour by hour, and day by day,
the please picture of the present fade,
and like a summer vapour steal away!

and have not they, who here forget lie
(say, hoary chronicler of age past!)
once mark thy shadow with delight eye,
nor think it flee, how certain, and how fast!

since thou have stand, and thus thy vigil keep,
note each hour, over moulder stone beneath;
the pastor and his flock alike have sleep,
and dust to dust proclaim the stride of death.

another race succeed, and count the hour,
careless alike; the hour still seem to smile,
as hope, and youth, and life, be in our power;
so smile and so perish the while.

i hear the village bell, with gladsome sound,
when to these scene a stranger i draw near,
proclaim the tiding to the village round,
while memory weep upon the good man bier. 

even so, when i be dead, shall the same bell
ring merry, when my brief day be go;
while still the lapse of time thy shadow tell,
and stranger gaze upon my humble stone!

enough, if we may wait in calm content,
the hour that bear we to the silent sod;
blameless improve the time that heaven have lend,
and leave the issue to thy will, o god!
