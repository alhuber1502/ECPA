to autumn.

sweet autumn! how the melancholy grace
steal on my heart, as through these shade i wind!
soothe by thy breathe sigh, i fond trace
each lonely image of the pensive mind!
love scene, love friend — long lose! around i rise,
and wake the melt thought, the tender tear!
that tear, that think, which more than mirth i prize —
sweet as the gradual tint that paint thy year!
thy farewell smile, with fond regret, i view,
thy beam light, soft glide over the wood;
thy distant landscape, touch with yellow hue,
while fall the lengthen gleam; thy wind flood,
now veil in shade, save where the skiff white sail
swell to the breeze, and catch thy stream ray.
but now, even now! — the partial vision fail,
and the wave smile, as sweep the cloud away!
emblem of life! — thus chequer be its plan,
thus joy succeed to grief — thus smile the vary man!
