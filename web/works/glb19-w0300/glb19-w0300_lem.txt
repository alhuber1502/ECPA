at OXFORD, 1786. 

bereave i not of fancy shadowy dream,
which win my heart, or when the gay career
of life begin, or when at time a tear
sit sad on memory cheek — though lofty theme
await the awaken mind to the high prize
of wisdom, hardly earn with toil and pain,
aspire patient; yet on life wide plain
leave fatherless, where many a wanderer sigh
hourly, and oft our road be lone and long,
it be not a crime shall we a while delay
amid the sunny field; and happy they
who, as they journey, woo the charm of song,
to cheer their way; — till they forget to weep,
and the tire sense be hush, and sink to sleep.
