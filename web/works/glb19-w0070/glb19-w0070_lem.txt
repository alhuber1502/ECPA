to the river itchin. 

itchin! when i behold thy bank again,
thy crumble margin, and thy silver breast,
on which the self-same tint still seem to rest,
why feel my heart a shiver sense of pain!
be it, that many a summer day have pass
since, in life morn, i carol on thy side!
be it, that oft since then my heart have sigh,
as youth, and hope delusive gleam, fly fast!
be it, that those who gather on thy shore,
companion of my youth, now meet no more!
whatever the cause, upon thy bank i bend,
sorrow; yet feel such solace at my heart,
as at the meeting of some long-lost friend,
from who, in happy hour, we weep to part.
