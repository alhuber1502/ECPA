to the BAT. 

from haunt of man, from day obtrusive glare,
thou shroud thou in the ruin ivy tower,
or in some shadowy glen romantic bower,
where visard form their mystic charm prepare,
where horror lurk, and ever-boding care!
but, at the sweet and silent evening hour,
when close in sleep be every languid flower,
thou love to sport upon the twilight air,
mock the eye, that will thy course pursue,
in many a wanton round, elastic, gay,
thou flit athwart the pensive wanderer way,
as his lone footstep print the mountain-dew.
from indian isle thou come, with summer car,
twilight thy love — thy guide her beam star!
