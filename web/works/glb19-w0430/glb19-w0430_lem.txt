to sir WALTER SCOTT.

on accident meeting and part with sir WALTER SCOTT, who i have not see for many year, in the street of LONDON, may 1828. 

since last i see that countenance so mild,
slow-stealing age, and a faint line of care,
have gentle touch, methinks, some feature there;
yet look the man as placid as a child,
and the same voice, — while mingle with the throng,
unknowing, and unknown, we pass along, —
that voice, a share of the brief time beguile!
that voice i never may hear again, i sigh
at part, — wheresoever our various way,
in this great world, — but from the bank of Tweed,
as slow sink the shade of even-tide,
oh! i shall hear the music of his reed,
far off, and think of that voice, shall say,
a blessing rest upon thy lock of grey!
