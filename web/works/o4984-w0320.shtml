ODE.

BY THE SAME.
1 WITH restless agitations toss'd,
2 And low immers'd in woes,
3 When shall my wild distemper'd thoughts
4 Regain their lost repose?
5 Beneath the deep oppressive gloom
6 My languid spirits fade:
7 And all the drooping powers of life
8 Decline to death's cold shade.
9 O Thou! the wretched's sure retreat,
10 These torturing cares controul,
11 And with the chearful smile of peace
12 Revive my fainting soul!
13 Did ever thy relenting ear
14 The humble plea disdain?
15 Or when did plaintive Misery sigh,
16 Or supplicate in vain?
[Page 159]
17 Opprest with grief and shame, dissolv'd
18 In penitential tears,
19 Thy goodness calms our restless doubts,
20 And dissipates our fears.
21 New life, from thy refreshing grace
22 Our sinking hearts receive;
23 Thy gentle, best lov'd attribute
24 To pity and forgive.
25 From that blest source propitious Hope
26 Appears serenely bright,
27 And sheds her soft diffufive beam
28 O'er Sorrow's dismal night.
29 Dispers'd by her superior force,
30 The sullen shades retire,
31 And opening gleams of new-born joy
32 The conscious soul inspire.
33 My griefs confess her vital power,
34 And bless the friendly ray:
35 Fair Phosphor to the smiling morn
36 Of everlasting day.
