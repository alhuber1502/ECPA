TO Mr. John Lock Retired from The World of Business.

I.
1 ANGELS are made of Heavenly Things,
2 And Light and Love our Souls compose,
3 Their Bliss within their Bosom springs,
4 Within their Bosom flows.
5 But narrow Minds still make pretence
6 To search the Coasts of Flesh and Sence,
7 And fetch Diviner Pleasures thence.
8 Men are akin to Ethereal Forms,
9 But they belye their Nobler Birth,
10 Debase their Honour down to Earth,
11 And claim a share with Worms.
[Page 118]
II.
12 He that has Treasures of his own
13 May leave the Cottage or the Throne,
14 May Quit the Globe, and dwell alone
15 Within his spacious Mind.
16 LOCK hath a Soul wide as the Sea,
17 Calm as the Night, bright as the Day,
18 There may his vast Idea's play,
19 Nor feel a Thought confin'd.
