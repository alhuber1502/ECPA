the piedmontese.

ah, merry swain, who laugh along the vale,
and with your gay pipe make the mountain ring,
why leave your cot, your wood, and thymy gale,
and friend belove, for aught that wealth can bring?
he go to wake over moon-light sea the string,
venetian gold his untaught fancy hail!
yet oft of home his simple carol sing,
and his step pause, as the last alp he scale.
once more he turn to view his native scene —
far, far below, as roll the cloud away,
he spy his cabin 'amid the pinetop green,
the wellknown wood, clear brook, and pasture gay;
and think of friend and parent leave behind,
of sylvan revel, dance, and festive song;
and hear the faint reed swell in the wind;
and his sad sigh the distant note prolong!
thus go the swain, till mountain shadow fall,
and dim the landscape to his ache sight;
and must he leave the vale he love so well?
can foreign wealth, and show, his heart delight?
no, happy vale! your wild rock still shall hear
his pipe, light sound on the morning breeze;
still shall he lead the flock to streamlet clear,
and watch at eve beneath the western tree.
away, venetian gold — your charm be over!
and now his swift step seek the lowland bower,
where, through the leaf, his cottage light once more
guide he to happy friend, and jocund hour.
ah, merry swain! that laugh along the vale,
and with your gay pipe make the mountain ring,
your cot, your wood, your thymy-scented gale —
and friend belove — more joy than wealth can bring!
