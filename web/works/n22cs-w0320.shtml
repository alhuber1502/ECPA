SONNET [32] XXXII. To Melancholy. Written on the Banks of the Arun, October, 1785.

1 WHEN latest Autumn spreads her evening veil
2 And the gray mists from these dim waves arise,
3 I love to listen to the hollow sighs,
4 Thro' the half leafless wood that breathes the gale.
5 For at such hours the shadowy phantom, pale,
6 Oft seems to fleet before the poet's eyes;
7 Strange sounds are heard, and mournful melodies,
8 As of night wand'rers, who their woes bewail!
9 Here, by his native stream, at such an hour,
10 Pity's own Otway, I methinks could meet,
11 And hear his deep sighs swell the sadden'd wind!
12 Oh Melancholy! — such thy magic power,
13 That to the soul these dreams are often sweet,
14 And soothe the pensive visionary mind!
