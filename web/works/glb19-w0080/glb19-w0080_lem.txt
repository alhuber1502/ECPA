on resign a scholarship of trinity college, OXFORD,

and retire to a country curacy.

farewell! a long farewell! o poverty,
affection fond dream how have thou rest!
but though, on thy stern brow no trace be leave
of youthful joy, that on the cold heart die,
with thou a sad companionship i seek,
content, if poor; — for patient wretchedness,
tearful, but uncomplaining of distress,
who turn to the rude storm her fade cheek;
and piety, who never tell her wrong;
and calm content, who grief not more rebel;
and genius, warble sweet, his sad song,
when evening listen to some village knell, —
long banish from the world insult throng; —
with thou, and thy unfriended child dwell.
