the prophecy.

i 

this truth of old be sorrow friend,
 "time at the worst will sure mend." 
the difficulty then to know,
 how long oppression clock can go;
 when britain son may cease to sigh,
 and hope that their redemption nigh. 

ii.

when vice exalt take the lead,
 and vengeance hang but by a thread;
 gay peeress turn out o'doors;
 whoremaster peer, and son of whore;
 look up, you briton! cease to sigh,
 for your redemption draw nigh. 

iii.

when vile corruption brazen face,
 at councilboard shall take her place;
 and lords-commissioner resort,
 to welcome she at britain court;
 look up, you briton! cease to sigh,
 for your redemption draw nigh. 

iv.

see pension harbour large and clear,
 defend by saint Stephen pier! 
the entrance safe, by current lead,
 tide round G—'s jetty head;
 look up, you briton! cease to sigh,
 for your redemption draw nigh. 

v. 

when civil-power shall snore at ease,
 while soldier fire — to keep the peace;
 when murder sanctuary find,
 and petticoat can justice blind;
 look up, you briton! cease to sigh,
 for your redemption draw nigh. 

vi.

commerce over bondage will prevail,
 free as the wind, that fill her sail. 
when she complain of vile restraint,
 and power be deaf to her complaint;
 look up, you briton! cease to sigh,
 for your redemption draw nigh. 

vii.

when raw projector shall begin,
 oppression hedge to keep she in;
 she in disdain will take her flight,
 and bid the Gotham fool good night;
 look up, you briton! cease to sigh,
 for your redemption draw nigh. 

viii.

when tax be lay, to save debate,
 by prudent minister of state;
 and, what the people do not give,
 be levy by prerogative;
 look up, you briton! cease to sigh
 for your redemption draw nigh. 

ix.

when popish bishop dare to claim
 authority, in George name;
 by treason hand set up, in spite
 of George title, William right;
 look up, you briton! cease to sigh,
 for your redemption draw nigh. 

x. 

when popish priest a pension draw
 from staryed exchequer, for the cause
 commission, proselyte to make
 in british realm, for britain sake;
 look up, you briton! cease to sigh,
 for your redemption draw nigh. 

xi.

when snug in power, sly recusant
 make law for british protestant;
 and d—g William revolution,
 as justice claim execution;
 look up, you briton! cease to sigh,
 for your redemption draw nigh. 

xii.

when soldier, pay for our defence,
 in wanton pride slay innocence;
 blood from the ground for vengeance reek,
 till heaven the inquisition make;
 look up, you briton! cease to sigh,
 for your redemption draw nigh. 

xiii.

when at Bute foot poor freedom lie,
 mark by the priest for sacrifice,
 and doom a victim, for the sin
 of half the out, and all the in;
 look up, you briton! cease to sigh,
 for your redemption draw nigh. 

xiv.

when steward pass a boot account,
 and credit for the gross amount;
 then to replace exhaust store,
 mortgage the land to borrow more;
 look up, you briton! cease to sigh,
 for your redemption draw nigh. 

XV. 

when scrutineer for private end,
 against the vote declare their friend;
 or judge as you stand there alive,
 that five be more than forty-five;
 look up, you briton! cease to sigh,
 for your redemption draw nigh. 

xvi.

when George shall condescend to hear
 the modest suit, the humble prayer;
 a prince, to purp'le pride unknown! 
no favourite disgrace the throne! 
look up, you briton! sigh no more,
 for your redemption at the door. 

xvii.

when time shall bring your wish about,
 or, seven-year lease, you sell, be out;
 no future contract to fulfil;
 your tenant hold at your will;
 raise up your head! your right demand
 for your redemption in your hand. 

xviii.

then be your time to strike the blow,
 and let the slave of Mammon know,
 britain true son a bribe can scorn,
 and die as free as they be bear. 
virtue again shall take her seat,
 and your redemption stand complete.
