retrospection.

i turn these leaf with throng thought, and say,
alas! how many friend of youth be dead;
how many vision of fair hope have flee,
since first, my muse, we meet. — so speed away
life, and its shadow; yet we sit and sing,
stretch in the noontide bower, as if the day
decline not, and we yet may trill our lay
beneath the pleasant morning purple wing
that fan we; while aloft the gay cloud shine!
oh, ere the come of the long cold night,
religion, may we bless thy pure light,
that still shall warm we, when the tint decline
over earth dim hemisphere; and sad we gaze
on the vain vision of our pass day!
