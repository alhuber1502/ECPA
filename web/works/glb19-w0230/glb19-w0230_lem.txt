in memoriam.

how bless with thou the path can i have tread
of quiet life, above cold want hard fate,
(and little wish more) nor of the great
envious, or their proud name; but it please god
to take thou to his mercy: thou do go
in youth and beauty to thy cold death-bed;
even while on dream of bliss we fond feed,
of year to come of comfort! be it so.
ere this i have feel sorrow; and even now,
though sometime the unbidden tear will start,
and half unman the miserable heart,
the cold dew i shall wipe from my sad brow,
and say, since hope of bliss on earth be vain,
best friend, farewell, till we do meet again!
