WRITTEN IN IRELAND.

1 How blest would be Iërne's isle,
2 Were bigotry and all it's guile
3 Chac'd as a cloud away;
4 Then would Religion rear her head,
5 And sweet Contentment round her spread,
6 Like a new dawn of day.
7 Come then, oh come, thou Truth divine!
8 With double radiance deign to shine,
9 Thy heavenly light expand;
10 'Tis thine to chase these clouds of night,
11 Which darken and confound the sight
12 In this divided land.
[Page 79]
13 Attendant on thy prosp'rous train
14 I see sweet Peace with honest gain
15 Spread wide her liberal hand,
16 While Discord, mask'd deep disguise,
17 Abash'd from forth her presence flies,
18 Struck by her magic wand.
19 Around, where now in ruins lie
20 Thy sacred altars, I espy
21 Fair Order rear each pile,
22 Whilst o'er thy wilds forlorn and waste,
23 Lo, Industry with nimble haste
24 Makes hill and valley smile.
25 No more thy sons in fell despite,
26 A murderous band array'd in white,
27 Shall deal destruction round;
28 Each man beneath his vine shall rest,
29 No more by Bigotry opprest,
30 But Truth by Peace be crown'd.
[Page 80]
31 Then shall Iërne tune her lyre,
32 And with united voice conspire
33 To hail her happy state;
34 All hail, Iërne, Nature's pride,
35 No more shall wars thy land divide,
36 Wert thou as good as great.
