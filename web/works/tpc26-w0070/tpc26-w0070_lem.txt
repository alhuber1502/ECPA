death of general Wolfe

to the publisher of the Pennsylvania magazine

set to music by a gentleman of this country, the word by Atlanticus

in a moulder cave, where the wretched retreat,
Britannia sit waste with care.
she weep for she Wolfe, then exclaim’d against fate,
and give herself up to despair.
the wall of her cell she have sculptur’d around
with exploit of her favourite son;
and even the dust as it lie on the ground,
be engrav’d with some deed he have do.

ii

the sire of the god from his crystalline throne,
behold the disconsolate dame;
and mov’d at her tear, he send mercury down,
and these be the tiding that come:
Britannia forbear, not a sigh, not a tear
for thy Wolfe so deserve lov’d;
your grief shall be change into triumph of joy,
for Wolfe be not dead but remov’d.

iii

the son of the earth, the proud giant of old,
have break from their darksome abode;
and such be the news, that in heaven ’tis tell,
they’re march to war with the god.
a council be hold in the chamber of Jove,
and this be the final decree,
that Wolfe shall be call’d to the army above,
and the charge be entrust to i.

iv

to the plain of Quebec with the order i fly,
he begg’d for a moment’s delay;
and cry, o forbear! let i victory hear,
and then the command i’ll obey.
with a darken film i encompass’d his eye,
and convey’d he away in a urn,
lest the fondness he bear to his own native shore,
shall tempt he again to return.
