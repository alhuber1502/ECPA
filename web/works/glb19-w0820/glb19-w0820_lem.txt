the harp, and despair, of Cowper.

sweet bard, who tone great Milton may approve,
and Shakspeare, from high fancy sphere,
turn to the sound his ear,
bend down a look of sympathy and love;
oh, swell the lyre again,
as if in full accord it pour a angel strain!
but oh! what mean that look aghast,
even while it seem in holy trance,
on scene of bliss above to glance!
be it a fiend of darkness pass!
oh, speak —
paleness be upon his cheek —
on his brow the big drop stand,
to airy vacancy
point the dread silence of his eye,
and the love lyre it fall, fall from his nerveless hand!
come, peace of mind, delightful guest!
oh, come, and make thy downy nest
once more on his sad heart!
meek faith, a drop of comfort shed;
sweet hope, support his age head;
and charity, avert the burn dart!
fruitless the prayer — the night of deep woe
seem over the head even now to close;
in vain the path of purity he tread,
in vain, in vain,
he pour from fancy shell his sweet hermit strain —
he have no hope on earth: forsake he not, o god!
