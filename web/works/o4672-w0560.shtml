THE REVERSE; ON THE View of some of my Friends remaining Comforts.

I.
1 THUS Nature tun'd her Mournful Tongue,
2 Till Grace lift up her Head,
3 Revers'd the Sorrow and the Song,
4 And smiling thus she said.
II.
5 Were kindred Spirits born for Cares?
6 Must every Grief be mine?
7 Is there a Sympathy in Tears,
8 And Joys refuse to Joyn?
[Page 165]
III.
9 Forbid it Heav'n, and raise my Love,
10 And make our Joys the same:
11 So Bliss and Friendship joyn'd above
12 Mix an Immortal Flame.
IV.
13 Sorrows are lost in vast Delight
14 That Brightens all the Soul,
15 As Deluges of dawning Light
16 O'rewhelm the Dusky Pole.
V.
17 Pleasures in long Succession reign
18 And all my Powers Imploy:
19 Friendship but shifts the pleasing Scene,
20 And fresh repeats the Joy.
VI.
21 Life has a soft and silver Thread,
22 Nor is it drawn too long,
23 Yet when my vaster Hopes perswade
24 I'me willing to be gone.
[Page 166]
VII.
25 Fast as ye please roll down the Hill,
26 And hast away, my Years;
27 Or I can wait my Father's Will,
28 And dwell beneath the Spheres.
VIII.
29 Rise glorious every future Sun,
30 And bright be all my Days,
31 Till Death that brightest Moment come
32 With well-distinguish't Rays.
