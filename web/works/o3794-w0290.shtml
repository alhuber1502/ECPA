HORACE. Book the 2d. ODE the 10th.

1.
1 RECEIVE, dear friend, the truths I teach,
2 So shalt thou live beyond the reach
3 Of adverse fortunes pow'r;
4 Not always tempt the distant deep,
5 Nor always timorously creep
6 Along the treach'rous shore.
[Page 333]
2.
7 He that holds fast the golden mean,
8 And lives contentedly between
9 The little and the great,
10 Feels not the wants that pinch the poor,
11 Nor plagues that haunt the rich man's door,
12 Imbitt'ring all his state.
3.
13 The tallest pines feel most the pow'r
14 Of wintry blasts, the loftiest tow'r
15 Comes heaviest to the ground,
16 The bolts that spare the mountains side,
17 His cloud-capt eminence divide
18 And spread the ruin round.
4.
19 The well inform'd philosopher
20 Rejoices with an wholesome fear,
21 And hopes in spite of pain;
22 If winter bellow from the north,
23 Soon the sweet spring comes dancing forth,
24 And nature laughs again.
[Page 334]
5.
25 What if thine heav'n be overcast,
26 The dark appearance will not last,
27 Expect a brighter sky;
28 The God that strings the silver bow,
29 Awakes sometimes the muses too,
30 And lays his arrows by.
6.
31 If hindrances obstruct thy way,
32 Thy magnanimity display,
33 And let thy strength be seen,
34 But oh! if Fortune fill thy sail
35 With more than a propitious gale,
36 Take half thy canvass in.
