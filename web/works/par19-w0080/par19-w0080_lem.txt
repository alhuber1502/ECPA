sunrise: a sonnet.

oft let i wander, at the break of day,
through the cool vale overhang with wave wood,
drink the rich fragrance of the bud may,
and catch the murmur of the distant flood;
or rest on the fresh bank of limpid rill,
where sleep the violet in the dewy shade,
where open lily balmy sweet distil,
and the wild musk-rose weep along the glade:
or climb the eastern cliff, who airy head
hang rude over the blue and misty main;
watch the fine hue of morn through æther speed,
and paint with roseate glow the crystal plain.
oh! who can speak the rapture of the soul
when over the wave the sun first steal to sight,
and all the world of water, as they roll,
and heaven vast vault unveil in live light!
so life young hour to man enchant smile,
with sparkle health, and joy, and fancy fairy wile:
