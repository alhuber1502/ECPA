upon a very ancient dutch house on LONG-ISLAND.

behold this antique dome by envious time,
grow crazy, and in every part decay;
full well, alas, it claim my humble rhyme,
for such lone haunt and contemplation make.

ah see the hearth, where once the cheerful fire!
blaze high, and warm the winter traveler toe;
and see the wall, which once do high aspire,
admit the storm, and every wind that blow.

in yonder corner, now to ruin go,
the ancient housewife curtained bed appear,
where she and her man JOHN do sleep alone,
nor nightly robber, nor the screach owl fear.
there do they snore full oft the whole night out,
smoke the sable pipe, until that do fall,
rest from their jaw by Somnus 'sleepy rout,
and on their face pour its scorch gall.

and in the compass of yonder small gang,
the swain batavian once his courtship make,
to some dutch lass, as thick as she be long;
"come then, my angel, come, the shepherd say,

"and let we for the bridal bed prepare;
for you alone shall case my future life,
and you alone shall soften all my care,
my strong, my hearty, and industrious wife."

thus they — but eat ruin now have spread
its wing destructive over the antique dome;
the mighty fabric now be all a shed,
scarce fit to be the wander beggar home.

and none but i it be piteous fate lament,
none, none but i over it be sad ash mourn,
send by the fate, and by Apollo send,
to shed their late tear upon it be silent urn.
