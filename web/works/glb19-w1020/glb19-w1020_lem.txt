battle of CORRUNA.

the tide of fate roll on! — heart-pierced and pale,
the gallant soldier lie, nor aught avail,
the shield, the sword, the spirit of the brave,
from rapine arm hand thy vale to save,
land of illustrious hero, who, of yore,
drench the same plain with the invader gore,
stand frown, in the front of death, and hurl
defiance to the conqueror of the world!

oh, when we hear the agonise tale
of those who, faint, and fugitive, and pale,
see hourly, harass through their long retreat,
some wear companion sink at their foot,
yet even in danger and from toil more bold,
back on their gather foe the tide of battle roll; —
while tear of pity mingle with applause,
on the dread scene in silence let we pause;
yes, pause, and ask, be not thy awful hand
stretch out, o god, over a devote land,
who vale of beauty nature spread in vain,
where misery moan on the uncultured plain,
where bigotry go by with jealous scowl,
where superstition mutter in his cowl;
while over the inquisition dismal hold,
its horrid banner wave in bleed fold!

and do thou thus, lord of all might, fulfil
with wreck and tempest thy eternal will,
Shatter the arm in which weak kingdom trust,
and strew their scatter ensign in the dust?
oh, if no human wisdom may withstand
the terror, lord, of thy uplift hand;
if the dark tide no prowess can control,
yet near, charge with dread commission, roll;
still may my country ark majestic ride,
though sole, yet safe, on the conflict tide;
till hush be the wild rock of the blast,
and the red storm of death be overpass!
