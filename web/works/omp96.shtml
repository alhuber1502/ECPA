    VERSES Humbly presented to the KING At His Arrival in HOLLAND, After the DISCOVERY Of the late horrid CONSPIRACY Against His most Sacred Person.

    By Mr. PRIOR.

    Serus in coelum redeas, diuque
    Laetus intersis populo Quirini,
    Neve te nostris vitiis iniquum
    Ocyor aura
    Tollat, hic magnos potius triumphos
    Hic ames dici pater atque Princeps,
    Neu sinas Gallos equitare inultos
    Te duce, CAESAR.
    Hor. ad Augustum.

    LONDON, Printed for Jacob Tonson, at the Judge's Head, near the Inner-Temple-Gate in Fleetstreet, 1696.
