to the wind.

viewless, through heaven vast vault, your course you steer,
unknown from whence you come, or whither go!
mysterious power! i hear you murmur low,
until swell your loud gust on my startle ear,
and, awful! seem to say — some god be near!
i love to list' your midnight voice float
in the dread storm that over the ocean roll,
and, while their charm the angry wave control,
mix with its sullen roar, and sink remote:
then, rise in the pause, a sweet note, —
the dirge of spirit, who your deed bewail, —
a sweet note, oft swell, while sleep the gale!
but soon, you sightless power! your rest be over:
solemn and slow you rise upon the air,
speak in the shroud, and bid the seaboy fear;
and the faint-warbled dirge — be hear no more!

oh! then i deprecate your awful reign! —
the loud lament yet bear not on your breath!
bear not the crash of bark far on the main;
bear not the cry of man, who cry in vain, —
the crew dread chorus sink into death!
oh! give not these, you power — i ask alone,
as, 'rapt, i climb these dark romantic steep —
the elemental war, the billow moan:
i ask the still, sweet tear, that listen fancy weep.
