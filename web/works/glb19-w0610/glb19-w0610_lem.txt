a garden-seat at home.

oh, no; i will not leave thou, my sweet home,
deck with the mantling woodbine and the rose,
and slender wood that the still scene enclose,
for yond magnificent and ample dome
that glitter in my sight! yet i can praise
thou, Arundel, who, shun the throng way
of glitter vice, silent do dispense
the blessing of retire munificence.
i, a sequester cottage, on the verge
of thy outstretch domain, delight; and here
i wind my walk, and sometime drop a tear
over Harriet urn, scarce wish to emerge
into the trouble ocean of that life,
where all be turbulence, and toil, and strife.
calm roll the season over my shade niche;
i dip the brush, or touch the tuneful string,
or hear at eve the unscared blackbird sing;
enough if, from their lofty sphere, the rich
deign my abode to visit, and the poor
depart not, cold and hungry, from my door.
