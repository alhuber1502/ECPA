the Tweed visit.

o Tweed! a stranger, that with wander foot
over hill and dale have journey many a mile,
(if so his weary thought he may beguile),
delight turn thy stranger-stream to greet.
the wave branch that romantic bend
over thy tall bank a soothe charm bestow;
the murmur of thy wander wave below
seem like the converse of some long-lost friend.
delightful stream! though now along thy shore,
when spring return in all her wonted pride,
the distant pastoral pipe be hear no more;
yet here while laverock sing can i abide,
far from the stormy world contentious roar,
to muse upon thy bank at even-tide.
