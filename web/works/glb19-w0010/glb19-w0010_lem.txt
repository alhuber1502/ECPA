at Tynemouth priory,

after a tempestuous voyage.

as slow i climb the cliff ascend side,
much muse on the track of terror past,
when over the dark wave ride the howl blast,
please i look back, and view the tranquil tide
that lave the pebble shore: and now the beam
of evening smile on the grey battlement,
and yond forsake tower that time have rend: —
the lift oar far off with transient gleam
be touch, and hush be all the billowy deep!
soothe by the scene, thus on tire nature breast
a stillness slow steal, and kindred rest;
while sea-sound lull she, as she sink to sleep,
like melody that mourn upon the lyre,
wake by the breeze, and, as they mourn, expire!
