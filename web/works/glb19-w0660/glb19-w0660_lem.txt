stranger! mark this lovely scene,
when the evening set serene,
and start over the silent wood,
the last pale sunshine streak the flood,
and the water gush near
sooth, with ceaseless drip, thy ear;
then bid each passion sink to rest; —
shall even one wish rise in thy breast,
one tender wish, as now in i,
that some such quiet spot be thy,
and thou, recall season flee,
can wake the slumber of the dead,
and bring back she you love, to share
with thou calm peace and comfort there; —
oh, check the thought, but inly pray
to he, "who give and take away,"
that many year this fair domain
its vary beauty may retain; —
so when some wanderer, who have lose
his heart best treasure, who have cross
in life bleak hill and pass rude,
shall gain this lovely solitude;
delight he may pause a while,
and when he mark the landscape smile,
leave with its willow, ere he part,
the blessing of a soften heart.
