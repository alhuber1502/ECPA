approach of summer.

how shall i meet thou, summer, wont to fill
my heart with gladness, when thy pleasant tide
first come, and on the coomb romantic side
be hear the distant cuckoo hollow bill!
fresh flower shall fringe the margin of the stream,
as with the song of joyance and of hope
the hedge-row shall ring loud, and on the slope
the poplar sparkle in the pass beam;
the shrub and laurel that i love to tend,
think their may-tide fragrance will delight,
with many a peaceful charm, thou, my poor friend,
shall put forth their green shoot, and cheer the sight!
but i shall mark their hue with sad eye,
and weep the more for one who in the cold earth lie!
