music.

o music! if thou have a charm
that may the sense of pain disarm,
be all thy tender tone address
to soothe to peace my Harriet breast;
and bid the magic of thy strain
so still the wakeful throb of pain,
that, rapt in the delightful measure,
sweet hope again may whisper pleasure,
and seem the note of spring to hear,
prelusive to a happy year!

and if thy magic can restore
the shade of day that smile no more,
and soft, sweet colour give
to scene that in remembrance live;
be to her pensive heart a friend,
and, while the tender shadow blend,
recall, ere the brief trace be lose,
each moment that she prize the most.

perhaps, when many a cheerful day
hereafter shall have steal away,
if then some old and favourite strain
shall bring back to her thought again
the hour when, silent by her side,
i listen to her song and sigh;
perhaps a long-forgotten name,
a thought, if not a tear may claim;
and when in distant plain away,
alone i count each linger day,
she may a silent prayer prefer
for he who heart once bleed for she.
