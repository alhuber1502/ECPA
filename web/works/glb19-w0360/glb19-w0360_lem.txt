Lacock Nunnery.

June 24, 1837. 

i stand upon the stone where Ela lie,
the widow founder of these ancient wall,
where fancy still on meek devotion call,
mark the ivy arch, and turret grey —
for her soul rest — eternal rest — to pray;
where visionary nun yet seem to tread,
a pale dim troop, the cloister of the dead,
though twice three hundred year have fly away! 
but when, with silent step and pensive mien,
in weed, as mourning for her sister go,
the mistress of this lone monastic scene
come; and i hear her voice tender tone,
i say, though century have roll between,
one gentle, beauteous nun be leave, on earth, alone.
