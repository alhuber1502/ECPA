HYMN III.

For EASTER-SUNDAY.

1 AGAIN the LORD of life and light
2 Awakes the kindling ray;
3 Unseals the eyelids of the morn,
4 And pours increasing day.
5 O what a night was that, which wrapt
6 The heathen world in gloom!
7 O what a sun which broke this day,
8 Triumphant from the tomb!
9 This day be grateful homage paid,
10 And loud hosannas sung;
11 Let gladness dwell in every heart,
12 And praise on every tongue.
[Page 119]
13 Ten thousand differing lips shall join
14 To hail this welcome morn;
15 Which scatters blessings from its wings,
16 To nations yet unborn.
17 JESUS, the friend of human kind,
18 With strong compassion mov'd,
19 Descended like a pitying GOD,
20 To save the souls he lov'd.
21 The powers of darkness leagued in vain
22 To bind his soul in death;
23 He shook their kingdom when he fell,
24 With his expiring breath.
25 Not long the toils of hell could keep
26 The hope of JUDAH's line;
27 Corruption never could take hold
28 On aught so much divine.
[Page 120]
29 And now his conquering chariot-wheels
30 Ascend the lofty skies;
31 While broke, beneath his powerful cross,
32 Death's iron sceptre lies.
33 Exalted high at GOD's right hand,
34 And LORD of all below,
35 Thro' him is pardoning love dispens'd,
36 And boundless blessings flow.
37 And still for erring, guilty man,
38 A brother's pity flows;
39 And still his bleeding heart is touch'd
40 With memory of our woes.
41 To thee, my Saviour, and my king,
42 Glad homage let me give;
43 And stand prepar'd like thee to die,
44 With thee that I may live.
