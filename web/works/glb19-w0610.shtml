A GARDEN-SEAT AT HOME.

1 Oh, no; I would not leave thee, my sweet home,
2 Decked with the mantling woodbine and the rose,
3 And slender woods that the still scene inclose,
4 For yon magnificent and ample dome50
50 Wardour Castle.
5 That glitters in my sight! yet I can praise
6 Thee, Arundel, who, shunning the thronged ways
7 Of glittering vice, silently dost dispense
8 The blessings of retired munificence.
9 Me, a sequestered cottage, on the verge
10 Of thy outstretched domain, delights; and here
11 I wind my walks, and sometimes drop a tear
12 O'er Harriet's urn, scarce wishing to emerge
13 Into the troubled ocean of that life,
14 Where all is turbulence, and toil, and strife.
15 Calm roll the seasons o'er my shaded niche;
16 I dip the brush, or touch the tuneful string,
17 Or hear at eve the unscared blackbirds sing;
18 Enough if, from their loftier sphere, the rich
19 Deign my abode to visit, and the poor
20 Depart not, cold and hungry, from my door.
Donhead, Oct. 12, 1798.
