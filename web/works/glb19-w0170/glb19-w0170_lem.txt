distant view of ENGLAND from the sea.

yes! from my eye the tear unbidden start,
as thou, my country, and the long-lost sight
of thy own cliff, that lift their summit white
above the wave, once more my beat heart
with eager hope and filial transport hail!
scene of my youth, revive gale you bring,
as when erewhile the tuneful morn of spring
joyous awake amid your hawthorn vale,
and fill with fragrance every village lane:
flee be those hour, and all the joy they give!
yet still i gaze, and count each rise wave
that bear i near to my home again;
if haply, 'amid those wood and vale so fair,
stranger to peace, i yet may meet she there.
