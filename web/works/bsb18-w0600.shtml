THE MEETING.

Air — Merrily danc'd the Quaker.
1 IF I hae been a week away,
2 My Jenny rins to meet me;
3 Wi' aw the chat o' this bit pleace
4 My Jenny's fain to treat me: —
[Page 215]
5 "There's Rob has married Mary Gray,
6 And Bella's past aw tellin!
7 And Greace has fun the little cat,
8 And Dick can say his spellin.
9 Peer Dick has broken deddy's dish,
10 And durstn't come to meet ye;
11 But he has sent ye this bit cake,
12 He thought that he mud treat ye.
13 Our butter tells to fourteen pun;
14 Our cheese hes fill'd the rimmer;
15 And uncle Megs hes sent us beef
16 Will sarra us aw at dinner.
17 And uncle Megs hes heard frae Gworge;
18 He's gane to — I've forgittin;
19 But it's some hard-word pleace owre seas,
20 I'll hae the neame on't written;
21 I think they caw'd it Jemmycaw,1.
1. Jamaica.
22 Or else it is St Christit;2.
2. St Christopher's, or, as the sailors call it, St Kit's.
23 And if it isn't yen o' they,
24 I' faikins, I hae mist it!
25 And peer auld Wully's telt his teale;
26 He'll never tell anudder!
27 And they've been up wi' uncle Megs,
28 To wreyte it till his brudder:
[Page 216]
29 For he was varra nwotishin
30 Of ought that Wully wanted;
31 And mony time wad wreyte and tell
32 They wadn't see him scanted.
33 They brought him varra canny up, —
34 He had the best o' linnen,
35 And keept it just to mense his death, —
36 'Twas peer auld Marget's spinnin.
37 The house, and aw the bits o' things,
38 Will just be for the brudder;
39 I only wish he'd meade tem owre
40 To Mary and her mudder! "
