a cenotaph,

to the memory of lieutenant-colonel Isaac, who die at cape saint Nichola Mole, 1797. 

oh, have thou fall, brave youth! on that proud day,
when our victorious fleet over the red surge
roll in terrific glory, thou have fall
most honour; and remembrance, while she think
upon thy gallant end, have dry her tear!
now far beyond the huge atlantic wave
thy bone decay; the wither pestilence,
that sweep the island of the western world,
smite thou, untimely droop to the tomb!
but it be enough; whatever a soldier fate,
that firm he hie he, where stern honour bid;
though with unequal strength, he sink and die.
