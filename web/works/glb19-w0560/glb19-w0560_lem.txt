perhaps, as here he muse: live, laurel green;
you pine that shade the solitary scene,
live bloom and rejoice! when i be dead
my son shall guard you, and amid your bower,
like i, find shelter from life beat shower.

these thought, my father, every spot endear;
and while i think, with self-accusing pain,
a stranger shall possess the love domain,
in each low wind i seem thy voice to hear.
but these be shadow of the shape brain
that now my heart, alas! can ill sustain:
we must forget — the world be wide — the abode

it boot not, so, to every chance resign,
wherever the spot, we bear the unaltered mind. 
yet, oh! poor cottage, and thou sylvan shade,
remember, ere i leave your covert green,
where in my youth i muse, in childhood play,
i gaze, i pause, i drop a tear unseen,
