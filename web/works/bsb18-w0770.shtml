I AM OF A TEMPER FIXED AS A DECREE.

1 I AM of a temper fixed as a decree,
2 Resolv'd with myself to live happy and free;
3 With the cares of this world I am seldom perplex'd,
4 I am sometimes uneasy, but never quite vex'd;
5 I am neither too high nor too low in degree;
6 There are more that live worse than live better than me.
7 My life thus moves on amid freedom and ease,
8 I go where I will, and I come when I please;
9 I am plac'd below envy, and yet above spite;
10 I've judgment enough still to do myself right:
11 Some higher, some lower, I own there may be,
12 But ambition and want are both strangers to me.
13 When money comes in, pleas'd I live till 'tis gone,
14 I am happy when with it, contented with none;
15 If I spend it 'mong friends I count it but lent,
16 It thus goes genteelly — I never repent;
17 With mirth to my labour the hours sweetly pass,
18 Though at Saturday night I am just where I was.
