THE BEAU MONDE, OR THE Pleasures of St. JAMES'S.

A BALLAD.

To the Tune of, Oh! LONDON is a fine-Town, &c.
1 OH! St. James's is a lovely Place,
2 'Tis better than the City;
3 For there are Balls and Operas,
4 And ev'ry Thing that's pretty.
5 There's little Lady CUZZONI,
6 And bouncing Dame FAUSTINA,
7 The Duce a Bit will either Sing
8 Unless they're each a QUEEN — a,
9 And when we've ek'd out History,
10 And made them Rival Queens,
11 They'll warble sweetly on the Stage,
12 And scold behind the Scenes:
Oh! St. James's, &c.
[Page 10]
13 When having fill'd their Pockets full,
14 No longer can they stay;
15 But turn their Backs upon the Town,
16 And scamper all away.
17 The Belles and Beaux cry after them,
18 With all their might and main;
19 And HEIDEGGER is sent in haste
20 To fetch 'em back again.
Oh! St. James's, &c.
21 Then Hey! for a Subscription
22 To th' Opera, or the Ball;
23 The Silver Ticket walks about
24 Untill there comes a Call.
25 This puts them into doleful Dumps,
26 Who were both blith and Gay;
27 There's nothing spoils Diversion more
28 Than telling what's to pay.
Oh! St. James's, &c.
29 There's POPE has made the witlings mad,
30 Who labour all they can;
31 To pull his Reputation down,
32 And maul the Little Man.
33 But Wit and he so close are link'd,
34 In vain is all this Pother;
35 They never can demolish one
36 Without destroying 'tother.
Oh! St. James's, &c.
[Page 11]
37 And there's Miss POLLY PEACHUM lugs
38 Our Nobles by the Ears,
39 'Till PONDER WELL by far Exceeds
40 The Musick of the Spheres.
41 When lo! to show the Wisdom Great
42 Of LONDON's famous Town,
43 We set her up above her self,
44 And then we take her down.
Oh! St. James's, &c.
45 And, there's your Beaux, with powder'd Cloaths,
46 Bedaub'd from Head to Shin;
47 Their Pocket-holes adorn'd with Gold,
48 But not a souse within:
49 And there's your pretty Gentlemen,
50 All dress'd in Silk and Sattin;
51 That get a Spice of ev'ry Thing,
52 Excepting Sense and Latin.
Oh! St. James's, &c.
53 And there's your Cits that have their Tits,
54 In Finsbury so sweet.
55 But costlier Tits they keep, God wot!
56 In Bond and Poultney-Street.
57 And there's your green Nobility,
58 On Citizens so witty,
59 Whose Fortune and Gentility,
60 Arose from LONDON's City.
Oh! St. James's, &c.
[Page 12]
61 We go to Bed when others rise,
62 And Dine at Candle-light;
63 There's nothing mends Complexion more,
64 Than turning Day to Night.
65 For what is Title, Wealth, or Wit,
66 If Folks are not Genteel?
67 Or how can they be said to live,
68 Who know not what's QUADRILLE.
Oh! St. James's, &c.
FINIS.
