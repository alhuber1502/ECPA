what be love?

’Tis that delightsome (full) transport we can feel
which painter can paint, nor word reveal
nor any art, we know of, can conceal.
can thou describe the sunbeam to the blind?
or make he feel a shadow with his mind?
so neither can we by description show
this first of all felicity below.
when happy love pour magic o’er the soul,
and all our thought in sweet delirium roll,
when contemplation spread its rainbow wing
and ev’ry flutter some new rapture bring,
how sweet then our moment glide away,
and dream repeat the transport of the day,
we live in ecstasy, to all thing kind,
for love can teach a moral to the mind.
but be there not some other mark that prove
what be this wonder of the soul, call’d love?
o yes there be but of a different kind,
the dreadful horror of a dismal mind.
some jealous fury throw her poison’d dart
and rend in piece the distract heart.
when love’s a tyrant, and the soul a slave,
no hope remain to thought — but in the grave.
in that dark den it see a end to grief,
and what be once its dread become relief.
what be the iron chain the hand have wrought?
the hard chain to break be make of thought.
think well of this, you lover, and be kind,
nor play with torture on a tortur’d mind.
