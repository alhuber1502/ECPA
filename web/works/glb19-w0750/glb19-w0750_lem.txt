winter evening at home.

fair moon, that at the chilly day decline
of sharp December through my cottage pane
do lovely look, smile, though in thy wane!
in thought, to scene, serene and still as thy,
wander my heart, while i by turn survey
thou slow wheel on thy evening way;
and this my fire, who dim, unequal light,
just glimmer, bid each shadowy image fall
sombrous and strange upon the darken wall,
ere the clear taper chase the deepen night!
yet thy still orb, see through the freeze haze,
shine calm and clear without; and while i gaze,
i think, around i in this twilight room,
i but remark mortality sad gloom;
while hope and joy cloudless and soft appear,
in the sweet beam that light thy distant sphere.
